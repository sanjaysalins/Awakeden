# Independent review — grok (OK, 141s)

**Findings (adversarial read, zero benefit of the doubt):**

**KJV verbatim — words inside quotes are now clean on the specific prior flags, but the selection and presentation still breach the artifact's own rule stated in DEPTH & SOURCING.**

The ledger claims: "every quote is either a COMPLETE KJV span or an honest PREFIX marked with a trailing ellipsis (…); NO words are ever omitted *inside* quotation marks."

This is not followed.

- MOVEMENT 4:  
  **[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 53:8]**  
  "for the transgression of my people was he stricken."  
  This is a trailing clause from the *end* of the verse (full KJV 53:8 ends "...for he was cut off out of the land of the living: for the transgression of my people was he stricken."). Lowercase "for", not a complete span, not a prefix, no ellipsis. Exact words inside the marks, but the selection rule the artifact sets for itself is broken.

- MOVEMENT 4:  
  **[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 53:9]**  
  "he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death; because he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth."  
  KJV actually opens the verse "And he made his grave...". Starts mid-verse, drops the "And", no marker. Same problem as above.

- MOVEMENT 2:  
  "his visage was so marred more than any man, and his form more than the sons of men" —  
  Mid-verse 52:14 (full: "As many were astonied at thee; his visage..."). Lowercase start, not prefix of the verse, no ellipsis.

- MOVEMENT 6:  
  **[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 53:10]**  
  "Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him; he hath put him to grief."  
  No trailing … even though the verse continues immediately ": when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin, he shall see his seed...". Truncated mid-verse without the marker the rule requires.

**53:10-11 "continuous verbatim, no interior ellipsis" claim (the exact thing the prior panel flagged) is still not clean in presentation.**

After the "grief." quote (no ellipsis), several lines of narration, then:

**[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 53:10-11]**  
"he shall see his seed, he shall prolong his days, and the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in his hand. He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied…"

In the actual text, between "grief:" and "he shall see his seed" sits "when thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin," (rest of v10). The label says 53:10-11 and the transition says "the chapter turns toward morning." A listener hears the "pleased to bruise" line then the seed/days/satisfied language with the connecting clause never voiced or marked. The ledger asserts this was fixed to continuous; the voiced excerpts still skip it.

**Hook and close import non-textual specifics from the crucifixion accounts, undercutting "surprising about the TEXT" and "biblically faithful".**

Cold open (first line):  
"Seven hundred years before a Roman nail was ever driven through a human wrist, a prophet in Jerusalem wrote down the death of a man he had never met."  
"the rich man who would own it."

Isaiah 52:13-53:12 never says "nail", "wrist", or "Roman". These are read in from John 20:25 / the passion narratives. "Rich man who would own it" is a traditional gloss on "with the rich in his death" (53:9), given before the verse is even quoted.

Later, MOVEMENT 7:  
"It has hands, wounded for transgressions that were never his own."

"hands" (plural) is not language from Isaiah 53. The text has "wounded", "bruised", "stripes". Consistent with the hook, this heightens with later details rather than staying inside what the passage itself says.

**Register break in MOVEMENT 6:**

"...the risen Servant feels one word: *satisfied.* It was enough. It worked."

"It was enough. It worked." is casual, almost slogan-like. It clashes with the claimed "reverent documentary pace" and the more careful sentence immediately before it ("a shape that only resurrection fills, a shadow the New Testament would bring into full light").

**Other specific issues under the lenses:**

- MOVEMENT 1 binary (first ~30s): "Either it is the most astonishing accident in all of human literature — or someone standing outside of time was telling us, centuries early, exactly how God would save the world."  
  Effective hook, but forces a two-option conclusion before the text walk and while the script itself later gives a serious treatment of the corporate reading in MOVEMENT 4. Not ideal for "clarity on first hearing" with zero Bible knowledge.

- MOVEMENT 4: "No people in history — not a nation, not even its most faithful remnant — has ever been honestly described that way."  
  This universal negative does heavy lifting to kill the Israel reading. The text describes *this Servant* as sinless; the "no people in history ever" is an extra-historical assertion the script adds.

- Landing: "Have you believed the report?"  
  It does thread work by circling back to 53:1 and avoids the exact generic "will you trust Him?" the spec forbids. Still functions as a direct 2nd-person challenge at the close of a "reverent documentary" long-form piece. The real new work is the "arm of the LORD... has your name in verse six" / "Already carried. Already paid in full." paragraph; the final question mostly re-asks the text's question.

- MOVEMENT 4: "A single grave. A rich man's tomb. A sinless life. This is not a people. This is a person."  
  "A rich man's tomb" is interpretive gloss on the quoted 53:9 ("with the rich in his death"), placed in the same list as the direct sinless claim from the text.

The specific prior KJV failures (Acts 8:35 elision, 53:10-11 interior splice, 49:3/53:3 punctuation, "pierced") are fixed in the words inside the marks. The broader discipline the artifact claims for itself on quote handling and "surprising about the TEXT" (not later details) is not.
