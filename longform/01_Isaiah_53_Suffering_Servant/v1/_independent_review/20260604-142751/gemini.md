The script’s theology, narrative spine, and grace-anchored landing are highly effective. The hook is gripping and the transition from the Israel-objection to the Ethiopian eunuch is exceptionally clear. 

However, you explicitly called this a "RE-LOCK pass" to confirm the KJV verbatim rules are clean. They are not. The script brazenly violates its own stated ledger rules ("COMPLETE KJV span or an honest PREFIX marked with a trailing ellipsis") in multiple places:

**1. Missing Trailing Ellipses on Prefixes**
The ledger strictly mandates that prefixes must be marked with a trailing ellipsis. The script fails to do this:
- **Movement 3 (Isaiah 53:4):** `"Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows"` is a prefix (KJV continues: `: yet we did esteem him...`). It entirely lacks the trailing ellipsis inside the quote.
- **Movement 6 (Isaiah 53:10):** `"Yet it pleased the LORD to bruise him; he hath put him to grief."` is a prefix (KJV continues: `: when thou shalt make...`). It lacks the trailing ellipsis. 

**2. Invented Punctuation**
The brief claims to have fixed an "invented period" in a prior pass, yet the script continues to invent terminal periods to replace KJV colons. This violates the "NO words [or characters] are ever omitted inside" strictly-verbatim rule:
- **Movement 6 (Isaiah 53:10):** Ends with an invented period (`grief.`) instead of the KJV colon (`grief:`).
- **Movement 5 (Acts 8:32):** Ends with an invented period (`mouth.`) instead of the KJV colon (`mouth:`).

**3. Silent Omission of Leading Words (Unmarked Mid-Verse Spans)**
The ledger claims quotes are "COMPLETE KJV spans", but the script repeatedly uses mid-verse suffixes without any leading ellipsis to indicate the omitted text, effectively altering the verbatim boundary:
- **Movement 4 (Isaiah 53:9):** Silently drops the opening "And" (KJV: "And he made..."). The quote just starts `"he made his grave..."`.
- **Movement 4 (Isaiah 53:8):** Silently drops the entire first half of the verse (KJV: "He was taken from prison and from judgment...").
- **Movement 5 (Acts 8:32):** Silently drops the opening clause (KJV: "The place of the scripture which he read was this,").
