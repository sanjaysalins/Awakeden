**FINDINGS**

**1. Clarity on first hearing (Audio-Logic Collision in Movement 5)**
*Cite:* "A man from Ethiopia — a court official — is riding home in his chariot, reading this very chapter aloud: **[narrator — KJV, Acts 8:32]** '…He was led as a sheep to the slaughter…'"
*Critique:* This is a jarring audio failure. The script explicitly tells the listener that the Ethiopian is reading "aloud," setting the expectation that we are about to hear the Ethiopian speak. Instead, the `[narrator]` reads the quote. Compounding the confusion, just two lines later, the actual `[eunuch]` voice appears to ask a question. To a listener, this will sound like a production mistake where the wrong voice actor read the line. If your locked Voice Plan mandates that the narrator read this KJV quote, you must change the setup line (e.g., "The scripture he was reading was this:") so you do not promise the ear an action it doesn't receive. 

**2. Faithful depth / Exegetical Integrity (Movement 1)**
*Cite:* "He described the wounds, the silence, the grave — and the rich man who would own it."
*Critique:* This projects the New Testament Gospel accounts backward onto the Old Testament text. Isaiah 53:9 says he made his grave "with the rich in his death," but it absolutely does not describe a rich man *owning* the tomb (that is Matthew 27:60). Claiming the prophet explicitly "described" the rich man owning it violates the mandate for faithful depth by over-reading the text to chase a neat, dramatic parallel. 

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix the audio dissonance in Movement 5: Either change the setup line so it doesn't explicitly state the Ethiopian is reading "aloud," or assign the Acts 8:32 quote to the `[eunuch]` voice.
2. Correct the exegetical over-reach in Movement 1: Remove the claim that Isaiah described a rich man "owning" the grave, keeping the phrasing strictly faithful to the text of verse 9.
