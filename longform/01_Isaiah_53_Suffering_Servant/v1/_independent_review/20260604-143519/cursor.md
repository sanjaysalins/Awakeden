## Doctrinal soundness

Mostly solid evangelical penal substitution, Acts 8 hinge, hedged resurrection.

**Pressure points:**

1. **M4 — `"No people in history — not a nation, not even its most faithful remnant — has ever been honestly described that way."`**  
   This is rhetoric, not exegesis. Corporate/remnant readers can treat 53:9 as idealized portrait or representative righteousness without being “dishonest.” You steel-man 49:3 well, then overstate the knockout.

2. **M4 — `"for the transgression of my people was he stricken"` → `"cannot simply *be* them."`**  
   Vicarious suffering *for* “my people” does not by itself prove the Servant is not Israel (a nation can be portrayed as suffering on behalf of its people). The argument works for **individual Messiah**, but the logic step is tighter than the script sounds.

3. **M4 — you cite 49:3 but not 49:5–6**  
   The stronger in-book answer is that the Servant is sent **to** Jacob/Israel (`"to bring Jacob again to him"`), distinct from Israel in the same passage you concede. Answering only from ch. 53 (and out of verse order: **53:9 before 53:8**) leaves the steel-man half-closed.

4. **M6 — after `"Isaiah does not spell out an empty tomb"`** you write **`"the risen Servant feels one word: *satisfied.*"`** then **`"It was enough. It worked."`**  
   Orthodox in intent, but you jump from careful hint to post-resurrection narrator voice and casual payoff (`"It worked."`) — slight tone/doctrinal whiplash for a “reverent documentary” brief.

No clickbait reading, no invented atonement theology. Philip → Jesus is NT-warranted.
