# Independent review — grok (OK, 140s)

**Findings (adversarial read, citing exact lines):**

**1. Self-description does not match the actual text on the claimed RE-LOCK fixes (core credibility problem).**

The header states: "v3 — KJV verbatim RE-LOCK (2026-06-04). ... Fixed: Acts 8:35 (now full verbatim "opened his mouth, and…"), Isaiah 53:10–11 (continuous verbatim, no interior ellipsis), 49:3 (full clause "…in whom I will be glorified."), 53:3 (trailing ellipsis, no invented period), "pierced"→"wounded" (M7, matches KJV 53:5)."

The DEPTH & SOURCING section repeats the claim: "49:3 (full clause "…in whom I will be glorified.")" as one of the fixes applied, and asserts "the independent panel confirmed all 17 spans" after the fixes.

But the narration itself in MOVEMENT 4 still uses the longer, pre-fix form:

**[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 49:3]**
"Thou art my servant, O Israel, in whom I will be glorified."

No leading ellipsis, and it includes "Thou art my servant, O Israel," instead of the short clause the ledger says was the fix. This is not a minor style note. The artifact's own "RE-LOCK pass 2" and "Fixed" record is factually wrong about what is actually on the page. An independent reviewer who only read the ledger would be auditing a different document than the one delivered.

**2. "Buried in verse nine; alive in verse eleven" states the conclusion too baldly before the walk-back (clarity + conservative framing failure).**

Exact line in MOVEMENT 6:

"Buried in verse nine; alive in verse eleven. Isaiah does not spell out an empty tomb — but he leaves a shape that only resurrection fills, a shadow the New Testament would bring into full light."

The first sentence hands the listener a direct "alive in verse eleven" claim. The qualification that protects the "hint not proof" stance only arrives in the next sentence. On first hearing (zero Bible knowledge listener), the strong assertion lands first. The ledger correctly notes the prior over-read was corrected by the external panel, but the spoken line still leads with the over-read and then hedges. This is the exact pattern the panel was supposed to have caught.

**3. "Already paid in full" introduces language and framing absent from the chapter (doctrinal wording drift).**

Exact line in MOVEMENT 7:

"Every way you turned that was your own — laid on him. Already carried. Already paid in full."

The surrounding phrases ("laid on him", "carried") are direct from 53:6. "Paid in full" is not. Isaiah 53 uses borne, carried, laid, wounded for, bruised, chastisement upon, stricken, bear their iniquities. "Paid in full" brings in a commercial/transactional satisfaction metaphor that is later theological gloss, not the text's own vocabulary. In a script that otherwise polices itself tightly on verbatim and "no invented doctrine," this is an unnecessary drift. It also slightly risks the very gain/loss or bookkeeping framing the grace-anchored rule is meant to exclude.

**4. The final landing question is closer to the generic form the lens prohibits than the artifact claims.**

The closing:

"The report is true. The arm of the LORD has a name. His name is Jesus.

Have you believed the report?"

The lens requires the landing to do "NEW work (not a tired generic 'will you trust Him?' close)." Re-asking the chapter's own opening question (53:1) is a deliberate bookend, and the "you do not have to clean yourself up before you come" sentence does real grace work. However, after six movements of deep textual argument, ending on a direct second-person "Have you believed the report?" functions as a standard "now the question is at your door" close. It is not tired in wording, but it is tired in function. The identification "His name is Jesus" does the new work; the question mostly reapplies pressure.

**5. Minor but real documentation/span claim that cannot be verified from the artifact alone.**

The DEPTH section claims "verified span-by-span against `data/kjv_cache.json` on 2026-06-04" and "the independent panel confirmed all 17 spans." The provided artifact gives no list of the 17 spans and no evidence of the cache check. Since the 49:3 ledger entry is already demonstrably disconnected from the text on the page, the blanket "all 17 confirmed" assertion is not self-authenticating. An adversarial reviewer treats this as an unverified claim until the actual spans + cache diffs are shown.

**Other notes (lower severity):**

- The Israel/corporate reading is steelmanned fairly (49:3 is quoted to give it its strongest text, then refuted from 53:9 and 53:8). This passes the "no sneer / contested reading for freshness" test.
- No actual altered KJV *words* inside quotation marks were found in the spoken lines (the 49:3 ledger mismatch is about which excerpt was chosen, not corrupted wording inside one). The Acts 8:35 fix is correctly rendered in the current text.
- Grace-anchored: the "you do not have to clean yourself up before you come" + "he was wounded so that you would not have to be" is strong and correctly placed. No fear/gain-loss/works pressure in the conviction.
- One thread spine holds: the "report" and "arm of the LORD" question opens and closes, the exchange (M3) is the center, the identity question (M4–5) and the resurrection shape (M6) serve it. No swapping.
- Hook (first ~5s) is effective for long-form: the 700-year anachronism + "wrote down the death of a man he had never met" + "what the wounds were *for*" sets up the astonishment well.

The artifact is careful in many places, but the mismatch between its revision history claims and its actual content, plus two lines that step past the conservative/verbatim discipline the rest of the piece advertises, are real problems.
