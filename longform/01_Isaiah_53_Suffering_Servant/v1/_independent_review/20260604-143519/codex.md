# Independent review — codex (OK, 186s)

**Findings**
- KJV spoken quote blocks: I found no altered KJV words. The repaired Acts 8:35 line, `"Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."`, is clean. Isaiah 52:14, 53:2-3, 53:4, 53:10-11, and Acts 8:32 are also word-clean under the stated excerpt rule.
- Textual overclaim in the hook: `"the grave — and the rich man who would own it"` goes beyond Isaiah’s wording. Isaiah says `"with the rich in his death"`; the owned tomb detail comes from NT fulfillment, not Isaiah 53 itself.
- Doctrinal/clarity overstatement: `"The arm of the LORD ... is not a thing. It is a person."` over-literalizes a biblical metaphor. Better theology would say the saving arm of the LORD is revealed in Jesus, not simply that the “arm” is a person.
- Landing imprecision: `"Already carried. Already paid in full."` before the faith question can sound like automatic personal acquittal apart from response. The CTA partially corrects this, but the line is too absolute for a zero-knowledge listener.
- Another landing risk: `"he was wounded so that you would not have to be"` can sound like a promise that believers will not suffer or be wounded. The intended point is penal substitution, but the wording can be heard as overpromised suffering-avoidance.
- Non-spoken ledger issue if audited literally: `"wounded for *our* transgressions… the LORD hath laid on him the iniquity of us all"` combines separate Isaiah lines inside one quote with an interior ellipsis. If the ledger is part of the artifact under the raw quote rule, clean it or remove it from scope.
