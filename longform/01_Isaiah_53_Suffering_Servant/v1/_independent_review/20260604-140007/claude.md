## Real findings (all minor / polish)

But the *strongest* in-context rebuttal sits two verses past your concession: 49:5-6, where the Servant has a mission *to* Israel (to "bring Jacob again to him"), so he is distinct from Israel *inside the same passage you conceded*. Quoting only the pro-Israel verse and leaving the chapter to answer it makes the concession feel one-sided. Not wrong — but a missed, stronger close.

**2. M1 hook — "a Roman nail ... through a human wrist."** Anatomically defensible, but it rubs against the very Scripture you honor ("they pierced my hands," Ps 22 / John 20). Opening line friction. Consider "hands" (or "wrists").

**3. M1 — "the rich man who would own it."** A zero-Bible listener doesn't yet know "it" = the grave; the pronoun foreshadows Joseph of Arimathea before they have the anchor. Tiny clarity snag. "the grave — and the rich man who would own *that grave*" fixes it.

**4. M7 — "The arm of the LORD ... is not a thing. It is a person."** The one interpretive identification (arm = the Servant = Christ). It's legitimate typology and the chapter's own flow supports it (the arm "revealed" *is* the Servant just described). Defensible — just the single line most exposed to a "says who?" challenge. Keep it tethered to the text's logic and it holds.
