# Independent review — gemini (OK, 151s)

This script is doctrinally ironclad and handles the "Israel as Servant" objection brilliantly by steel-manning the text rather than relying on a strawman. However, your execution betrays the "reverent documentary" mandate by slipping into mechanical, classroom-lecture transitions, and you undercut your strict KJV framing with an unconscious translation bleed at the climax. 

Here are the specific failures:

**1. Translation Bleed at the Climax (M7)**
In the landing, the narrator says: *"It has hands, pierced for transgressions that were never his own."* 
You spent the entire script strictly establishing the KJV vocabulary (*"he was wounded for our transgressions"* in M3). "Pierced" is the ESV/NIV translation of Isaiah 53:5. By swapping vocabulary at the emotional peak, you break the exact resonance you spent 6 minutes building. Echo the KJV precisely: use "wounded."

**2. Clunky, Essayistic Phrasing (M4)**
*"Here is what the chapter does that no nation can survive."*
This is an illogical and clunky sentence. A chapter doesn't "do" things a nation can't survive. It pulls the listener out of the narrative. Reword to address the claims of the text: *"But the prophet includes details no nation can survive,"* or *"But the text makes demands..."*

**3. Mechanical, "Page-Turning" Transitions (M3 & M4)**
You are writing a documentary, not pointing at a whiteboard, yet your transitions rely on structural geography: *"Then comes the centre of the chapter..."* (M3) and *"Then watch how he suffers."* (M4). These seams pull the listener out of the story. Pivot on the *meaning* of the events, not the layout of the page (e.g., instead of pointing out the center of the chapter, transition with: *"But this suffering is not random. Every line is a transaction."*).

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix translation bleed in M7: change "pierced" to "wounded" to maintain strict resonance with the established KJV text.
2. Rewrite the clunky audio phrasing in M4 ("Here is what the chapter does...").
3. Replace the mechanical, classroom-style transitions at M3 and M4 with narrative-driven pivots that maintain the documentary immersion.
