## KJV verbatim (hard flags)

**1. Acts 8:35 — not verbatim inside the quote marks**

Spoken line:
> `"Then Philip... began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."`

KJV (Acts 8:35): *"Then Philip **opened his mouth**, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."*

The ellipsis replaces real KJV words. That is an altered quote, not a legal fragment. The DEPTH ledger claim *"All quoted Scripture is KJV verbatim"* is false for this line.

**2. Isaiah 49:3 — terminal punctuation wrong**

Spoken:
> `"Thou art my servant, O Israel."`

KJV continues with a comma: *"Thou art my servant, O Israel**,** in whom I will be glorified."*  

Swapping KJV’s comma for a period is exactly the `kjv-verbatim-punctuation` failure mode your engine already tracks.

**3. Isaiah 53:2–3 — terminal punctuation on v.3**

Spoken ends:
> `"...acquainted with grief."`

KJV 53:3 ends:
> `"...acquainted with grief**:** and we hid as it were our faces from him..."`

Period for colon is a mismatch. You dropped the second half of v.3 (fine for pacing after the prior “We hid…” incident), but the closing punctuation on what remains is still wrong under a strict gate.

**4. Mid-verse fragments (lower severity, still note)**

These are word-accurate substrings but omit verse openings/closings: 52:14 (no *"As many were astonied at thee;"*), 53:4 (cuts before *": yet we did esteem him stricken..."*), 53:8–9 (no *"And he"* / full v.8). Acceptable for long-form teaching **only if** your gate treats them as fragments; the metadata should not claim “all quotes verbatim” without that qualifier.

**5. Isaiah 53:10–11 splice**

> `"he shall see his seed, he shall prolong his days... He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied."`

Words are mostly exact, but the ellipsis deletes *"and the pleasure of the LORD shall prosper in his hand"* and the rest of v.11. Same category as Acts 8:35: not a full KJV line as labeled.

## Doctrinal soundness

**Mostly evangelical and text-anchored.** Penal substitution comes from 53:4–6, not invention. Acts 8 bridge is standard. M6’s *"a shape that only resurrection fills"* is appropriately hedged vs. the older panel draft’s harder claim.

**Pressure points:**

- **M4 sinlessness slam:** *"No people in history — not a nation, not even its most faithful remnant — has ever been honestly described that way."*  
  That is rhetoric, not exegesis. Corporate/remnant readings treat 53:9 as idealized portrait or representative righteousness, not a census of Israel’s moral record. You steel-man 49:3 well, then answer with an argument a serious Jewish reader can still reject without being dishonest.

- **M4 “suffers *for* → cannot *be*”:** *"for the transgression of my people was he stricken"* / *"The Servant stands in their place — so he cannot simply **be** them."*  
  Logically tight for individual Messiah reading; less airtight against corporate reading (the nation as vicarious representative suffering *for* “my people”). You don’t strawman, but you don’t fully close the strongest comeback.

- **M7 narrator adds “pierced”:** *"It has hands, **pierced** for transgressions that were never his own."*  
  Isaiah says *wounded* / *bruised*, not pierced. Orthodox Christology, but a zero-Bible listener may think that word came from the chapter you just quoted.

- **M6 temporal slip:** *"And looking back over all of it, the **risen** Servant feels one word: *satisfied.*"*  
  Two sentences earlier you rightly hedge resurrection from bare Isaiah; then you narrate from a post-resurrection vantage without signaling “already in NT light.” Slight doctrinal whiplash, not heresy.

## Clarity on first hearing

**Strong:** M1–3 transaction language, M5 Acts 8 story, M7 explanation of *“arm of the LORD.”*

**Weaker:**

- **M1 binary:** *"Either it is the most astonishing accident... or someone standing **outside of time**"* — abstract for a stranger; sounds like a logic trick before you’ve earned trust.

- **M4 out-of-order citations:** You quote **53:9 before 53:8** while saying “First… Second…” A careful listener who knows the chapter will feel the scramble; a newcomer may not care, but it’s a small coherence tax.

- **M6 “It worked.”** Colloquial after heavy *“pleased the LORD to bruise him”* theology — sounds like a mechanism report, not worship.

## Grace-anchored conviction

**Good core:** *"You do not have to clean yourself up before you come"* and substitution framed as God’s act, not self-improvement.

**Friction:**

- **M3:** *"takes what they had **earned**"* — edges toward wages/merit framing (even if theologically defensible for “earned condemnation”).

- **M7:** *"Already **paid in full**"* — debt-settlement commerce on top of grace line; constitution explicitly dislikes gain/loss / transaction vibes in landing beats.

No overt fear-selling; no “try harder.” The commercial verbs are the main grace risk.
