# Independent review — grok (OK, 119s)

The narration violates its own "KJV verbatim" rule in multiple labeled quotation blocks.

In MOVEMENT 5 the block tagged **[narrator — KJV, Acts 8:35]** reads:  
"Then Philip... began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."  

The actual KJV is: "Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus." Words inside the quotation marks have been removed and replaced with ellipsis. This is not verbatim.

In MOVEMENT 4 the block tagged **[narrator — KJV, Isaiah 53:9]** reads:  
"he made his grave with the wicked, and with the rich in his death; because he had done no violence, neither was any deceit in his mouth."  

The actual KJV begins "And he made his grave..." The initial "And" is dropped and the following word is lowercased inside the quotation marks.

Other blocks present mid-verse excerpts under full verse citations without indicating the omitted prior words in the same verse (e.g., the Isaiah 52:14 block in MOVEMENT 2 drops "As many were astonied at thee;" and begins directly with the continuation casing). The DEPTH & SOURCING ledger claims "All quoted Scripture is KJV verbatim" and that "Independent red-team + external panel verified all quotes verbatim," yet the delivered script text contradicts that claim on the page.

The hook opens with material absent from the text:  
"Seven hundred years before a Roman nail was ever driven through a human wrist, a prophet in Jerusalem wrote down the death of a man he had never met."  

Isaiah 52:13–53:12 describes marring, wounding, bruising, stripes, being cut off, and pouring out the soul unto death. It names no execution instrument, no "nail," no "Roman," and no body part "wrist." These specifics are imported from later Gospel accounts and Roman crucifixion practice. The claimed surprise is therefore not generated from the passage itself.

The same importation occurs in the landing (MOVEMENT 7):  
"It has hands, pierced for transgressions that were never his own."  

Isaiah 53 never uses the word "hands" or the verb "pierced" for the Servant. The chapter uses "wounded," "bruised," "stripes," "oppressed and afflicted," and "cut off out of the land of the living." "Pierced" language for the hands comes from other OT texts (commonly Zechariah 12:10) and the NT crucifixion narratives. The script attributes the detail directly to the figure described in Isaiah 53 as part of identifying "the arm of the LORD."

MOVEMENT 6 states:  
"Pleased. Not because the Father delights in pain — but because He delights in what the cross would accomplish..."  

The source passage does not contain the word "cross." The execution device is unnamed in Isaiah 52–53. Using "the cross" here reads the later historical fulfillment back into the explanation of "it pleased the LORD to bruise him."

The final lines of MOVEMENT 7 are:  
"The report is true. The arm of the LORD has a name. His name is Jesus.  
Have you believed the report?"  

The preceding sentence ("You do not have to clean yourself up before you come") performs the required grace-anchored invitation. The terminal question, however, simply re-asks the text's own opening rhetorical question as a direct second-person prompt to the listener. This is the generic belief-decision close the criteria explicitly flags as insufficient. The landing does not do distinctly new work with the specific content of the substitution or the "arm" identification; it falls back on the text's own question.

MOVEMENT 6 also states:  
"And looking back over all of it, the risen Servant feels one word: *satisfied.* It was enough. It worked."  

Isaiah 53:10–11 uses future tense: "he shall see his seed, he shall prolong his days... He shall see of the travail of his soul, and shall be satisfied." The text does not present the Servant looking back, experiencing present emotion ("feels"), or rendering the reflective verdict "It was enough. It worked." This is post-resurrection dramatic narration layered onto the prophetic statement.

The central interpretive claim in MOVEMENT 7 creates first-hearing clarity risk for the stated audience (zero Bible knowledge):  
"The arm of the LORD — the saving strength of God — is not a thing. It is a person. It has a face, marred more than any man's. It has hands, pierced for transgressions that were never his own."  

The chapter asks "to whom is the arm of the LORD revealed?" immediately after the report of the Servant, but it never equates the "arm" with the Servant in explicit terms or describes the arm as having a face and pierced hands. The script presents the personification and the physical details as direct description rather than as a later fulfillment identification that must be argued. A listener hearing this cold has to track an unstated logical bridge in real time. This is the "clever-but-confusing conceit" the criteria prohibits.

The phrase "a face beaten past recognising" (MOVEMENT 2) is slightly non-idiomatic. The conventional English expression is "beyond recognition."

"someone standing outside of time" (MOVEMENT 1) is vague in a piece that otherwise names "the LORD" and maintains doctrinal precision. It leaves open the possibility of a third party rather than identifying the speaker as God.

The thread spine (report → arm of the LORD bookend, exchange as center, "of whom" answered by Jesus, return to the report) is held without swapping. The steel-manning of the corporate-Israel reading in MOVEMENT 4 is textually disciplined. The grace sentence "you do not have to clean yourself up before you come" is clean and non-transactional. These elements are not the primary defects.
