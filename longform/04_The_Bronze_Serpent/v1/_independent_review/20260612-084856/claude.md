## Doctrine — sound, with one phrase to watch
The serpent-type is guarded correctly: *"Christ did not become a sinner. He took the curse"* (M5), with God as the subject of 2 Cor 5:21. Good.

**Flag (M5):** *"He was lifted up *as* our poison."* This phrase sits one sentence after you just insisted "Christ did not become a sinner." Calling Christ "our poison" — even as the serpent/likeness metaphor — risks blurring the very distinction you're protecting, especially on first hearing. The type is "likeness of sinful flesh" / "made a curse," not "became the venom." Recommend softening to "lifted up in the likeness of our curse" or similar.

## Contestable claim presented as fact (M4)
*"And the very next sentence Jesus speaks is the one the whole world knows: 'For God so loved the world…'"* — Whether John 3:16 is **Jesus' own speech** or **John's narrated commentary** is a genuine, unsettled question (many editions/scholars end Jesus' red-letter words at v.15). You assert it confidently as "the very next sentence Jesus speaks." That's a freshness-driven overreach into contested ground. Safer: "And the passage flows straight into the verse the whole world knows…" — keeps the punch without staking a disputed claim.
