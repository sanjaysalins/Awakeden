## Visual reading
The arc opens on the empty tomb at dawn — 'it doesn't end in a grave' — then turns to the psalm scroll moving from the dark cross-lines to the bright praise of verse 22. The proof movement reveals the risen Christ alive, the One whose voice the praise-line becomes, and the Hebrews citation that names Him. The conviction is the risen Christ unashamed to call us brethren — His wounded hand on a brother's shoulder, the gathered family. The landing is the risen Christ with arms open, welcoming the listener in. The hero close is the welcoming risen Christ — the gospel-pivot of this resurrection short.

Resurrection must be NT-warranted: the visual turn (cross -> empty tomb -> risen Christ among brethren) follows the Hebrews citation, not a bare-psalm over-read. Two scroll/David scenes (2,3,12) kept distinct. The hero is the WELCOMING risen Christ (gospel-pivot CTA), not the most triumphant pose. No bare-torso on the risen figure (robed, for clean animation).
