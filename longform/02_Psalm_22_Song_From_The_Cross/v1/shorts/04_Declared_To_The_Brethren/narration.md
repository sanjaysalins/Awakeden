**[narrator]**
Psalm twenty-two is the psalm Jesus quoted from the cross. Most people stop reading it there — and miss where it actually ends.

**[narrator]**
But the cry doesn't stay forsaken. Later, the psalm turns — the same voice moves from anguish to praise:

**[narrator — KJV, Psalm 22:22]**
"I will declare thy name unto my brethren: in the midst of the congregation will I praise thee."

**[narrator]**
Who is that praising voice? Hebrews takes that opening line — word for word — and puts it in the mouth of the risen Christ. The psalm's turn is Jesus, alive on the far side of the cross.

**[narrator]**
And hear what He calls us. Hebrews says He is —

**[narrator — KJV, Hebrews 2:11]**
"not ashamed to call them brethren"

**[narrator]**
— brothers. Family.

**[narrator]**
The same Jesus who cried out forsaken now lives — not ashamed to call all who come to Him His own. That's where it all lands: not a grave, but a family. Come to Him.

## DEPTH & SOURCING  *(not spoken)*
- **KJV verbatim** (self-verified via `pipeline/scripture.fetch_kjv`, 2026-06-06):
  - Ps 22:22: "I will declare thy name unto my brethren: in the midst of the congregation will I praise thee." — exact, full verse.
  - Heb 2:11: "not ashamed to call them brethren" — exact KJV phrase (the verse: "…he is not ashamed to call them
    brethren,"). "them" is the KJV wording; the narrator applies it to "us/you" outside the quote.
- **Heb 2:12 is REFERENCED, not re-quoted:** "puts that very line in the mouth of the risen Jesus." The shared
  phrase "I will declare thy name unto my brethren" is identical in Ps 22:22 and Heb 2:12, so "that very line" is
  honest. NOT re-quoted because (a) it would repeat the line just heard, and (b) Heb 2:12's tail follows the
  Septuagint ("in the midst of the church will I sing praise") which DIVERGES from the psalm's KJV
  ("congregation… praise thee") — conflating them would be inaccurate.
- **Resurrection is NT-WARRANTED, not over-read:** the claim that the v22 turn = resurrection rests on Hebrews
  2:12 explicitly putting the verse in the RISEN Christ's mouth (an actual NT citation/application), plus the
  psalm's own structural pivot (the forsaken voice of vv1-21 stands alive praising in vv22-31). This is the
  long-form's careful framing, compressed — NOT a resurrection "proved" from bare Psalm 22.
- **Grace:** Heb 2:11 "not ashamed to call them brethren" — the forsaken-then-risen One brings us into the family.
  No fear/gain-loss. CTA-to-Jesus ("Come to Him"). Landing does new work (the turn: grave → family).
- **"why hast thou forsaken me" callback** is PARAPHRASED ("cried out forsaken"), not quoted — keeps to 2 quoted
  spans and ties back to short #3.
