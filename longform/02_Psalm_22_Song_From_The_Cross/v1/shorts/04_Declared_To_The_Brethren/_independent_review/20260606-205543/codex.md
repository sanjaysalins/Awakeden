# Independent review — codex (OK, 55s)

Findings:

1. **Hebrews/Psalm conflation risk:** “**that very line**” comes right after the full Psalm 22:22 quote. Hebrews 2:12 does not reproduce the whole KJV line exactly; only the opening phrase matches. This can make listeners think Hebrews quotes the whole Psalm 22:22 wording verbatim.

2. **Resurrection claim leans too much on bare Psalm logic:** “**A dead man declares nothing. So the New Testament takes that very line…**” sounds like the resurrection is being inferred by a clever syllogism from Psalm 22 itself. The safer warrant is Hebrews’ application to the risen/glorified Christ, not “a dead man declares nothing.”

3. **Possible universalizing of family language:** “**unashamed to call you His own**” states the status directly to every listener before the CTA. “Come to Him” helps, but the line still risks making Hebrews’ “them brethren” sound automatically true of all hearers rather than offered to those who come to Christ.

4. **KJV quotations themselves pass:** “**I will declare thy name unto my brethren: in the midst of the congregation will I praise thee.**” is exact KJV Psalm 22:22. “**not ashamed to call them brethren**” is exact KJV wording from Hebrews 2:11.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Change “that very line” to specify the shared opening phrase, not the whole Psalm 22:22 sentence.
2. Reframe “A dead man declares nothing. So…” so the resurrection claim rests explicitly on Hebrews’ risen-Christ application.
3. Qualify “call you His own” as an invitation/offer to those who come to Christ.
