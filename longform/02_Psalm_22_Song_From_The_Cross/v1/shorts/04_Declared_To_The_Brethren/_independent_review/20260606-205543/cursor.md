## Independent adversarial review — *Declared to the Brethren* (Psalm 22 short #4)

### KJV verbatim — **PASS (spoken quotes only)**

Both quoted spans match project KJV cache exactly:

- Ps 22:22: *"I will declare thy name unto my brethren: in the midst of the congregation will I praise thee."*
- Heb 2:11 fragment: *"not ashamed to call them brethren"* — exact substring of KJV Heb 2:11.

No altered words inside quotation marks. Paraphrases outside quotes (*"cried out forsaken"*, *"from death"*) are not presented as KJV.

---

### Doctrinal soundness — **mostly sound; one spoken-order problem**

The NT claim is orthodox: Hebrews applies Ps 22:22 to the risen Christ; Heb 2:11’s *brethren* language is used correctly. No universalism if *"Come to Him"* is read as invitation.

**Residual issue — resurrection is still argued from the psalm before the NT warrant lands:**

1. *"Later, the psalm turns — from death to a man standing alive, praising God in a crowd"* — states resurrection as psalm fact **before** Hebrews is named.
2. *"A dead man declares nothing. So the New Testament takes that very line…"* — the proof step is psalm-only rhetoric; Hebrews follows as confirmation (*"So…"*).

That inverts your stated guard: resurrection should be **NT-warranted via Hebrews**, not inferred from bare v22. Lament psalms often pivot to vowed praise **before** deliverance; an *"I will praise"* line does not require a dead man to be speaking. The long-form panel flagged this same move; this short improves by voicing Hebrews, but **does not fix the order or the "dead man" logic**.

*"from death"* is also interpretive compression — v22 does not say *death*; vv1–21 describe agony/near-death, not a settled corpse-to-life turn on the psalm’s face alone.

---

### Clarity on first hearing — **minor friction**

- **Hook (series #4):** *"Psalm twenty-two is the psalm Jesus quoted from the cross"* assumes cross-quote context from short #3. Fine in-sequence; weak if this clip is heard cold (no psalm number, no quote named).
- **NT citation unnamed on first use:** *"So the New Testament takes that very line"* — a zero-Bible listener gets no anchor (*Hebrews*, *risen Christ*) until the next beat. Hebrews is named only at *"Hebrews says He is —"*.
- **Quote splice:** *"Hebrews says He is —"* + *"not ashamed to call them brethren"* — grammatically awkward on one breath; listener must supply *"He is"* mentally. Not KJV error, but choppy first hearing.

---

### Grace-anchored conviction — **PASS**

No fear, gain-loss, works, or pressure framing. *"Come to Him"* invites; *"not ashamed to call them brethren"* is grace-shaped family language.

**Small precision note:** *"unashamed to call **you** His own"* applies Heb 2:11’s *"them"* (the sanctified) directly to the listener **before** the CTA. Paired with *"Come to Him"* this can read as offer, but it still blurs *"them who are his"* vs. every hearer already counted family. Softer than universalism, but worth tightening if accuracy is #1.

The Hebrews *brethren* / psalm-turn pairing is genuinely text-driven, not clickbait. The Septuagint tail divergence is handled correctly in metadata (Ps 22:22 quoted, Heb 2:12 referenced not re-quoted). Good discipline.

### Summary

KJV and core doctrine hold. The genuine residual flaw is **hermeneutical sequencing**: the script still **proves resurrection from v22** (*"standing alive"*, *"A dead man declares nothing"*) and only then cites the NT — despite metadata claiming the opposite. That is the same class of issue the long-form external panel flagged, only partially remedied here.

---

VERDICT: REVISE  
TOP FIXES:  
1. Reorder the resurrection turn: name **Hebrews 2:12** first (*"Hebrews puts this psalm on the lips of the risen Christ"*) and treat v22’s pivot as **confirmed by** that citation — drop or soften *"A dead man declares nothing"* as psalm-only proof.  
2. Soften pre-Hebrews psalm language: replace *"from death to a man standing alive"* with structural pivot language (*turn from forsaken cry to praise in the congregation*) until the NT warrant speaks.  
3. Tighten Heb 2:11 application: keep KJV *"them"* in the quote, then frame *"you"* as invitation (*"to those who come to Him"*) so *"call you His own"* doesn’t sound like accomplished status for every hearer before the CTA.
