A thousand years before a Roman cross, a king wrote a song describing a death he would never die.

**[narrator]**
Psalm twenty-two. David wrote it in the first person — but David himself was never executed; he died an old man. He was describing someone else: stripped, surrounded, his life poured out.

**[narrator]**

**[narrator — KJV, Psalm 22:18]**

**[narrator]**

## DEPTH & SOURCING  *(not spoken — synced to v4)*
- **KJV verbatim** (self-verified via `pipeline/scripture.fetch_kjv`, 2026-06-06): Ps 22:18 (full verse, exact);
  John 19:24's phrase "that the scripture might be fulfilled" quoted exactly (the surrounding line is narrator
  paraphrase). These are the ONLY quoted spans — the proof rests entirely on them.
- **Why no "pierced" (22:16):** the Masoretic text reads כָּאֲרִי ("like a lion") vs the Septuagint/standard
  "they pierced" — a genuine textual dispute. Two 5-CLI panels showed that even a demoted pierced line still
  carried rhetorical weight and re-opened the dispute. So for a 60s short where accuracy is #1, **it was removed**:
  the case stands on the UNDISPUTED garments detail, which John 19:24 EXPLICITLY cites as fulfilled. The full
  pierced steel-man lives in the backing long-form (Movement 4); a dedicated pierced short would carry the variant.
- **The convergence is honest:** Ps 22:18 itself names TWO acts — "part my garments" AND "cast lots upon my
  vesture" — and John 19:24 records the soldiers doing both (dividing the clothes, casting lots for the seamless
  coat). "Two acts… both, to the letter" reflects the verse's own parallelism, not an inflated count.
- **Accuracy of framing:** "a thousand years before a Roman cross" (David ~1000 BC; avoids the disputed
  "invented/perfected crucifixion" claim). "David… lived to old age" (1 Kings 2:10) — the first-person sufferer
  is explicitly NOT David. No claim the listener's literal name is "in" the text.
- **Grace-anchored landing:** no fear/gain-loss; "promised a thousand years before He came, and given for you.
  Come to Him." ("given for you" is gospel-invitation register — Luke 22:19 / Gal 2:20 — not a forced
  penal-substitution argument. The earlier volition overclaim "walked to it on purpose" was removed in v5,
  since the garments prophecy shows foreknowledge of what was done TO Him, not His own volition.)
- **Funnel:** distilled from the locked Psalm 22 long; inherits its verified research. This is the 🟢 airtight
  garments short (#1 by cross-ref strength) from the Psalm 22 short-series map.
