# Scene plan — Independent red-team audit

**Verdict:** `LOCKED`  ·  **Failed gates:** 0

## Visual reading
The arc opens on the careless hands gambling for a dying man's clothes - the prophecy made visible before it is explained - then steps back a thousand years to the aged King David writing it by lamplight, a death plainly not his own. The middle is forensic: the exact written line, the seamless coat, the soldiers casting lots at the foot of Christ's cross. It rises to the gospel pivot - the foretold cross held in reverent stillness - and lands on Christ's face and outstretched arms: not an accident, but a life laid down on purpose to win the viewer back. Bleak dusk and lamplit shadow throughout, warming only at the close.

## Writer's own red-team notes
The Jaded Viewer would expect a generic Golgotha skyline and a glowing scroll - so the hook is a tight, grubby close of dice and stripped cloth instead, and the scroll appears as a worked, cracked-ink macro, not a halo. Risk of slideshow: countered with five unified vignette scenes carrying the argument (contrast, fulfilment, the wider execution, the plan, the thousand-year bridge). The biggest doctrinal trap is the pierced variant (22:16) - visually tempting but deliberately removed from this short; a candidate for it is listed as REJECTED so the proof stays on the undisputed garments. Joseph's coat is an obvious OT-echo auto-complete but the NT never cites it here - rejected. The cross appears several times; framings are varied (low-angle hero, mid revelation, close face, mid devotional) so it does not read as one repeated shot.

## Scenes
- **1. The Dice in the Dust** ★ `[single/close/opening-hook]`
  · purpose: Open on the thread made visible - the careless gambling for a dying man's clothes.
  · rationale: Opens on Psalm 22:18's anchor act (divide + cast lots), not a generic cross; the cliche auto-complete would be a wide Golgotha skyline.
  · visible: soldiers' hands, two tumbling knuckle-bone dice, a heap of stripped garments, a seamless coat set apart, the foot of a wooden upright
- **2. David at the Lamp** ★ `[single/mid/ot-echo]`
  · purpose: Ground the prophecy - the aged king writing words larger than his own life.
  · rationale: Shows the OT author a thousand years early; rejects the harp-playing shepherd-boy auto-complete for an old king writing.
  · visible: aged King David, iron stylus, half-unrolled parchment scroll with wet Hebrew ink, oil lamp, a gold circlet set aside
- **3. A Death Not His Own** `[unified/wide/public-misunderstanding]`
  · purpose: Make the scandal visible - David dies safe while describing an execution.
  · rationale: Carries the point beat as one painting; the contrast (peaceful king vs executed sufferer) is the argument.
  · visible: aged David peaceful on a deathbed, with shadowed background vignettes of a stripped sufferer, a jeering crowd, a poured-out body, a far cross
- **4. The Line, Centuries Early** ★ `[single/close/theological-centre]`
  · purpose: Proof - the exact written sentence about garments and lots.
  · rationale: The verse itself is the evidence; a macro of the line lets the cut planner push into the words.
  · visible: open Psalm scroll, a fingertip beneath one illuminated Hebrew line, cracked old ink, lamplit parchment
- **5. The Coat They Would Not Tear** `[single/close/symbolic-support]`
  · purpose: Proof detail - John's seamless coat, the garment they cast lots for.
  · rationale: Names the specific John 19 detail (the coat without seam) that the dividing-and-lots prophecy turns on.
  · visible: a single seamless tunic held taut between two soldiers' hands, woven without seam, a blurred cross beam behind
- **6. Cast Lots at the Cross** ★ `[unified/mid/nt-gospel-link/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: Proof - the fulfilment at Christ's cross, John 19:24.
  · rationale: The NT moment the prophecy lands on; Christ present above keeps the gospel link explicit.
  · visible: soldiers crouched dividing garments and casting lots at the foot of the cross, Christ's lower robe and feet just in upper frame
- **7. The Cross, Foretold** ★ `[single/low-angle/nt-gospel-link/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: The gospel pivot - the foretold death, the CTA image the short lands on.
  · rationale: HERO: iconic, near-still, reverent; bookends the cut and carries the landing to Christ.
  · visible: the crucified Christ on a rough cross against a darkening sky, robed at the waist, soldiers tiny below over a heap of garments
- **8. The Whole Execution, Written Early** `[unified/wide/ot-echo]`
  · purpose: Deepen the precision - Psalm 22's wider crucifixion imagery, written centuries early.
  · rationale: Uses 22:12-15 (bulls, lion, dogs, poured out) as the OT text's own picture of the death; no typological reach.
  · visible: open Psalm scroll on stone, shadowed vignettes of bulls compassing, a roaring lion, dogs ringing a man, a body poured out
- **9. A Life Down to a Pile of Cloth** `[single/close/symbolic-support]`
  · purpose: Proof texture - the stripped clothing itself, the object of the prophecy.
  · rationale: Grounds the abstract garments in a tactile heap; a strong macro source for the cut.
  · visible: a heap of stripped garments on bloodstained stone, a leather belt and sandals tangled in the cloth
- **10. No Accident - the Plan** `[unified/mid/revelation/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: Conviction - the cross was written down, not stumbled into.
  · rationale: Carries the conviction beat: Christ sovereign and calm, the prophecy in shadow behind him.
  · visible: the crucified Christ in sovereign stillness, darkened noon sky, shadowed vignettes of an open scroll, dividing soldiers, a darkened sun, an inscription
- **11. A Thousand Years Apart** `[unified/wide/theological-centre/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: Bridge the centuries - the scroll and the cross as one composition.
  · rationale: Makes the central claim spatial: one sentence reaching from David's lamp to Christ's cross.
  · visible: aged David at his lamp on one side, the crucified Christ small on a far hill on the other, joined by shadow
- **12. Chance Rolls Out a Certainty** `[single/close/symbolic-support]`
  · purpose: A single-macro punctuation shot of the falling lots.
  · rationale: Insert-grade macro; the dice are the hinge between chance and prophecy.
  · visible: extreme close of two knuckle-bone dice mid-tumble above a paving stone
- **13. His Name Is Jesus** ★ `[single/close/revelation/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: Landing - recognising the suffering man's name.
  · rationale: The face turns the prophecy personal; close and reverent for a near-full-speed hold.
  · visible: a close reverent view of the crucified Christ's face, thorn-marked brow, eyes lowered in peace
- **14. Laying Down His Life** `[single/mid/closing-devotional/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: Landing - the life laid down on purpose to win you back.
  · rationale: Opens the frame to light; the gospel invitation as a held, reverent close.
  · visible: the crucified Christ from a respectful distance, arms along the beam, a soft break of light behind the hill
