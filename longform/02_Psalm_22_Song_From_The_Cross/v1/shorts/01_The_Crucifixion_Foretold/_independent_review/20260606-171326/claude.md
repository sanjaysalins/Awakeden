## Real findings (specific)

**1. The freshness now rests entirely on "Two precise acts, written centuries early" — and that's the one line a skeptic will hit.**
Ps 22:18 is Hebrew *synonymous parallelism*: "part my garments / cast lots upon my vesture" is arguably **one act stated twice poetically**, not two distinct predictions. The "two acts" only become two because **John 19:24** distinguishes dividing the outer clothes from casting lots for the seamless coat. So the precision is John's reading of the psalm, not self-evidently the psalm's. This is the **standard evangelical reading** and is defensible — but the script asserts the psalm itself "wrote" two acts. Safer/honest framing: the psalm's doubled line landed as two literal events at the cross. As written it's an overclaim a panel reviewer can challenge.

**2. "pierced" is demoted in placement but still load-bearing for the climax.**
"The same psalm even says: '…they pierced my hands and my feet.' / **Long before the nails**, God had written the rescue down." The nails beat — the emotional payoff — leans on the disputed word. It's softer than v2, and the undisputed proof precedes it, so it's acceptable. But "pierced" isn't truly just "one corroborating closing line"; it still drives the cross image. Mild.
