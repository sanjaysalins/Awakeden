# Independent review — codex (OK, 95s)

**Findings**
- `"that the scripture might be fulfilled."` is not strictly KJV-verbatim as quoted. The words match John 19:24, but KJV continues with a comma, not a period: this clipped quote is presented as a complete sentence. Fix the punctuation or quote the fuller clause. Sources checked: [Ps 22:18](https://biblehub.com/kjv/psalms/22-18.htm), [Ps 22:16](https://kjv.sh/psalms/22/16), [John 19:24](https://www.biblestudytools.com/kjv/john/19-24.html).

- `"…they pierced my hands and my feet."` uses exact KJV words, but the spoken line still makes the disputed reading a punch-up proof beat: `The same psalm even says:`. It is demoted from v2, but still rhetorically used for force without any signal that the undisputed, John-cited garment detail is carrying the argument.

- `It was the plan, with your name in it.` is the weakest doctrinal/clarity line. Psalm 22 and John 19 support divine fulfillment and personal gospel invitation; they do not say the listener’s individual name was “in” the plan. This risks sounding invented or self-centered.

- `But David died old, in his own bed.` is clear, but “in his own bed” is more specific than the biblical death notice clearly gives. If accuracy is #1, avoid concrete scene details that are inferred rather than stated.

- The spine is mostly coherent: hook → Psalm 22 → garments → John’s fulfillment → Jesus. The landing, though, partly collapses into generic CTA with `Come to Him.` The stronger landing is already `The suffering man of Psalm twenty-two has a name: Jesus`; the final invitation should do more text-specific work.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix the John 19:24 quotation so the KJV quote is not clipped with non-KJV punctuation.
2. Further reduce or carefully frame the disputed Psalm 22:16 “pierced” line so garments remain the honest proof.
3. Replace `with your name in it` and `in his own bed` with tighter, text-grounded wording.
