### Convergence honesty — materially improved, one residual risk

v3 does what v2 failed to do:

- **Garments lead:** first quoted detail is 22:18, not pierced.
- **John attached to the right detail:** `"John records it at the cross: soldiers split Jesus' clothes, then gamble for the last piece — 'that the scripture might be fulfilled.'"` — antecedent of *it* is garment-division/gambling, not pierced hands.
- **Pierced demoted:** `"The same psalm even says"` frames 22:16 as corroboration, not primary proof.
- **Disputed word removed from conviction/landing** — no `"Those pierced hands were planned"` / `"pierced for you"`.

**Residual risk:** `"Two precise acts, written centuries early"` sits immediately before the pierced quote. A rushed listener can still hear *garments + pierced* as the “two acts,” even though the prior sentence only named divide + gamble. The changelog says v3 fixed this to `"two exact acts: divided, and gambled for"` — **that explicit naming is not in the spoken text.** Precision was promised, not delivered.

**Adversarial question:** If accuracy is #1 and garments + John carry the argument, why quote the most textually contested line at all in a 60s cut? Even demoted, it re-opens the exact dispute v2 was revised to downplay.

### Doctrinal soundness — mostly PASS, two soft overreaches

Orthodox Messianic Psalm 22 reading; no contrarian text-critical hot take in spoken lines; Davidic attribution is standard.

**Overreach 1:** `"God had written the rescue down"` — *rescue from what?* The proof block establishes foretold suffering and garment-detail fulfillment, not rescue accomplished. Evangelically fine, but the noun outruns what was just proven.

**Overreach 2:** `"He went to that cross on purpose — for you"` — moves from predictive prophecy to personal substitution without a connecting beat. Orthodox landing, but the logic jump is real for a zero-Bible listener.

### Clarity on first hearing — REVISE (improved hook, muddy middle)

**Hook — PASS:** `"a king wrote a song describing a death he would never die"` + `"But David died old, in his own bed"` fixes v2’s self-contradiction. Strong, honest tension.

**Voice/person confusion — still a problem:**

- Beat 2: `"David, in the first person, describing a man stripped, surrounded…"`
- Beat 3: `"the man's killers would divide his clothes"`
- Beat 4 (KJV): `"They part **my** garments…"`

A zero-Bible listener now tracks **three voices**: David the writer, “a man” in third person, and first-person *my* in the quote. Who is suffering — David, another man, or Jesus? The landing names Jesus, but **no bridge explains why the first-person voice isn’t David’s own death.** `"The suffering man of Psalm twenty-two has a name: Jesus"` is cleaner than v2’s `"name written all the way through it,"` but still a leap, not a step.

**`"with your name in it"`** — unclear on first hearing. Your literal name? Personal salvation? The listener’s place in the plan? It pivots from objective prophecy proof to subjective personalization with no on-ramp. Risks sounding clever rather than clear.

**`"Two precise acts"`** — acts never named aloud; listener must infer divide + gamble from the prior sentence.
