# Independent review — codex (OK, 56s)

Findings:

- **KJV precision issue:** `"…they pierced my hands and my feet."` The words are KJV, but the ellipsis is inside the quotation marks and is not KJV text. If the rule is strict “inside quotation marks must be exact KJV,” this fails formatting discipline. Put the ellipsis outside the quote or quote the full verse/clause.

- **Possible overstatement / first-hearing confusion:** `a man described his own execution — in the first person` and `David, writing as though it were his own body: a man stripped, surrounded, and killed.` David was not executed. Psalm 22 is a first-person lament prophetically fulfilled in Christ, but this wording can sound like David literally narrated his own death.

- **The “convergence” claim is weaker in the spoken script than in the notes:** `One detail could be coincidence. Not this many, written this early.` In the narration, the only hard details are pierced hands/feet and gambled garments. Since “pierced” is the known contested word, the spoken proof still feels more dependent on it than the brief claims.

- **Potentially confusing poetic close:** `Psalm twenty-two has a name written all the way through it.` A zero-knowledge listener could hear this as mystical or literal, as though Jesus’ name appears in the psalm. The intended meaning is orthodox, but the phrase is too clever for first hearing.

- **John 19 could be more precise:** `John says it happened so "the scripture might be fulfilled."` The quoted words are KJV, but the narration does not say that John is specifically citing the garment-lot verse. Since the short rests on that undisputed convergence, this should be clearer.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Remove the ellipsis from inside the KJV quotation or quote the full KJV clause/verse.
2. Soften “his own execution” / “killed” so David is not made to sound literally executed.
3. Make John’s explicit fulfillment of Psalm 22:18 carry the proof more clearly than the disputed “pierced” wording.
