# Independent review — codex (OK, 18s)

Findings:

1. **Overstatement / doctrinal precision risk:** “**it never happened to him; he died an old man**” is too absolute. Psalm 22 can arise from David’s real suffering while reaching prophetic fulfillment in Christ. Better narrow the claim to the specific death scene: this death was not David’s.

2. **Clarity issue:** “**soldiers split Jesus' clothes, then threw dice for the rest**” makes “the rest” vague. John’s detail is that they divided the garments and cast lots for the coat/tunic. “The rest” could sound like they gambled for leftover clothes generally.

3. **Potentially inflated certainty:** “**Both, to the letter**” is mostly defensible, but with the prior paraphrase “threw dice for the rest,” it risks sounding more precise than the narration has actually shown. If you keep “to the letter,” make the John detail more exact.

4. **Hook is strong:** “**A thousand years before a Roman cross...**” grips quickly and stays on the garments-prophecy spine. No thread-swap.

5. **KJV quoted spans look exact:** “**They part my garments among them, and cast lots upon my vesture.**” matches KJV Psalm 22:18. “**that the scripture might be fulfilled**” matches the KJV phrase in John 19:24.

6. **CTA is grace-anchored:** “**He walked to it on purpose, for you. Come to Him.**” avoids fear, gain-loss, and works framing. It lands the prophecy as purposeful grace, not merely apologetic proof.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Replace “it never happened to him” with a narrower claim about that death not being David’s.
2. Make John 19:24’s garment detail clearer than “the rest.”
3. Tighten “Both, to the letter” so it rests on the exact divide/cast-lots parallel.
