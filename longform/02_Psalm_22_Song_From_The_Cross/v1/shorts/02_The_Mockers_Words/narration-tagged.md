<speaker name="narrator">A thousand years before the cross, David wrote down the words Jesus' enemies would hurl at Him — and the way they'd shake their heads doing it. Psalm twenty-two. David, describing a mocked and dying man, even writes his tormentors' words: "…they shake the head, saying, He trusted on the LORD that he would deliver him: let him deliver him…" A thousand years later, at the cross, Matthew records both — the passers-by wagging their heads, and the religious leaders jeering: "He trusted in God; let him deliver him now…" The same gesture. The same taunt — flung at Him by people who never saw what they were echoing. "Let him deliver him," they sneered. And He wasn't delivered — not because Jesus lacked the power to come down; He had every power. He stayed because staying was the only way to deliver you. They thought weakness pinned Him to that cross. It was love. That's Jesus — come to Him.</speaker>
