**Verdict:** `LOCKED`  ·  **Failed gates:** 0

## Visual reading
The arc opens on the mockers' faces at the foot of the cross — shot-out lips, shaking heads — then steps back a thousand years to David recording those very gestures and words on his scroll. The proof movement intercuts the two scenes: the Hebrew taunt and the cross-side crowd doing exactly what was written. The conviction turns the camera up to Christ, sovereign and unhurried, the power He held back rendered as half-dissolved restrained legions. The landing rests on His face looking down in love and the full cross 'with you in view' — the close lands on the crucified Christ, the gospel-pivot.

## Writer's own red-team notes
Jaded Viewer risks: a generic 'angry crowd' could read as any mob — so the mockers are anchored to the SPECIFIC Psalm 22:7 actions (shot-out lip, shaken head, pointed finger), not vague rage. Two scroll scenes (2, 12) risk overlap — kept distinct (David writing vs the thousand-years diptych). The 'restrained legions' scene (9) risks fantasy-glow angels — anchored as half-dissolved shadow-memory, not a glowing host. The hero must be the cross, not the most emotional face — so #11 (full crucified Christ) is hero, with #10 (face in love) as the emotional climax feeding it.
