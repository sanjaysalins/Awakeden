### Genuine residual issues (specific lines)

**1. Matthew “both” is only half-proven in audio — first-hearing gap**

> `Matthew records both — the passers-by wagging their heads, and the priests and elders jeering:`  
> then only Matt 27:43 is quoted.

The taunt is demonstrated in KJV audio; the gesture is narrator paraphrase only. The next line then asserts:

> `The same gesture. The same taunt`

A zero-Bible listener just heard *one* Matthew quote and must take the gesture match on trust. This is the same structural weakness the v2 panel flagged; v3 fixed the thread-swap but not the evidence asymmetry. Not heresy or KJV failure — but a real clarity soft spot at the proof beat.

**2. `He wasn't delivered` — micro-beat stumble before the denial lands**

> `"Let him deliver him," they sneered. And He wasn't delivered — not because Jesus lacked the power…`

For a fraction of a second this can sound like the narrator agreeing with the mockers’ premise (God/Jesus failed to rescue) before the em-dash correction. Unlike v1’s `They were right`, the repair is immediate and explicit — so this is **not** a doctrinal regression. Still a first-hearing speed bump in the most load-bearing sentence.

**3. `deliver you` — object unspecified for a cold listener**

> `staying was the only way to deliver you`

The mockers’ `deliver` means *rescue from the cross*. The landing pivots to `deliver you` without naming *from what* (sin, death, judgment). The wordplay is textually honest and orthodox, but a listener with zero Bible background may not follow the referent shift. Minor, not fatal.

**4. KJV presentation nit (only if punctuation/casing are strict)**

- Leading/trailing `…` inside quote marks are excerpt markers, not KJV text (house style usually accepts this; strict “quotes = KJV only” would move ellipses outside).
- Landing echo `"Let him deliver him,"` capitalizes `Let` — sentence-initial convention, not KJV casing (`let him deliver him`).

Neither alters wording; flag only if your gate treats casing/presentation as hard fail.
