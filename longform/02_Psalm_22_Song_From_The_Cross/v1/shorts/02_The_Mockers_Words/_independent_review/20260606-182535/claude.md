## Real flags (none are heresy or KJV errors — these are first-hearing/polish)

**1. Line 5 carries two stumbles for a zero-Bible listener.**
"They dared **the Father** to take Him down. **He didn't** — not because **Jesus** lacked the power to step off."
- "the Father" is introduced cold. A true zero-knowledge listener may not connect "the Father" = the "God" they just heard quoted. Whose father?
- Then the pronoun pivots: "He didn't" = the Father; next clause reasons about Jesus's power. Two agents (Father / Son) blur in one breath.
- This is a deliberate trade-off you made (you swapped "God didn't" → "the Father" to dodge abandonment). It works doctrinally, but it costs first-hearing clarity — the lens explicitly demands zero-Bible follow-through. Tighten so the voluntary-choice subject is unmistakable.

**2. Hook vs. your own guard — mild internal tension.**
Hook: "David **wrote down the words** Jesus' enemies **would hurl at Him**." That's strong predictive-prophecy framing — yet your ACCURACY GUARD insists this is "a MATCH, never a fulfilled-citation." Worth a conscious check that you're comfortable with the hook's confidence.

**3. "He trusted in God" → "They dared the Father."** Substituting "the Father" for the quoted "God" is an interpretive move (orthodox, and it does aid Father/Son distinction). Just confirm it's intended — same root as flag #1.
