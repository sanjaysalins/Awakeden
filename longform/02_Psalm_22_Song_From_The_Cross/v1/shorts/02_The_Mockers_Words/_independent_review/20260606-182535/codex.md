# Independent review — codex (OK, 58s)

Findings:

- KJV issue: Psalm quote uses ellipses inside the quotation marks: `"…they shake the head, saying, He trusted on the LORD that he would deliver him: let him deliver him…"`. The KJV words are correct against Psalm 22:7-8, but the ellipses are not KJV text. If the house rule is strict “everything inside quotes is KJV,” move ellipses outside or mark it as an excerpt. Source checked: Bible.com KJV Psalm 22:7-8; BibleGateway KJV Matthew 27:42-43.

- Pronoun / Trinitarian clarity problem: `"They dared the Father to take Him down. He didn't — not because Jesus lacked the power to step off. He had it. He stayed..."` switches from the Father as “He” to Jesus as “He” across four short clauses. On first hearing, that is muddy. Say explicitly: “The Father did not take Him down. Not because Jesus lacked power. Jesus had it.”

- Possible overclaim of inner knowledge: `"from people who never knew they were echoing it."` Matthew does not tell us what the mockers did or did not know. Some leaders were Scripture-literate. Better: “from people who did not see what they were echoing” or “without meaning to.”

- Slight source-label concern: `"the rulers jeering"` while claiming “Matthew records both.” Matthew names “chief priests… with the scribes and elders,” not simply “rulers.” “Rulers” is defensible shorthand, but for an accuracy-first short, use Matthew’s own categories or “religious leaders.”

- Landing no longer concedes inability. `"But it wasn't that He couldn't — He wouldn't"` clearly denies the dangerous v1 implication. Doctrinally, this is much safer.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Clarify the Father/Jesus pronoun switch in “He didn't… He had it. He stayed.”
2. Remove or move the ellipses inside quoted KJV text.
3. Replace “never knew they were echoing it” with a claim Matthew actually supports.
