# Independent review — codex (OK, 112s)

Checked against KJV Psalm 22:7-8, Matthew 27:39-43, and Matthew 26:53. Sources: [Psalm 22:7-8 KJV](https://www.bible.com/bible/1/PSA.22.7-8.KJV), [Matthew 27:39-43 KJV](https://biblia.com/bible/kjv1900/matthew/27/39-43), [Matthew 26:53 KJV](https://biblia.com/bible/kjv1900/matthew/26/53).

Findings:

1. Textual attribution is too compressed: “Matthew describes the crowd ‘wagging their heads’ and sneering:” Matthew 27:39 gives the head-wagging to “they that passed by”; Matthew 27:41-43 gives “He trusted in God…” to chief priests, scribes, and elders. Say Matthew’s crucifixion scene contains both gesture and taunt, not that one “crowd” does both.

2. “They dared God to rescue Him — and God didn’t” is dangerous on first hearing. Without “by taking Him down from the cross,” it can sound like God simply failed or refused to rescue Jesus, ignoring resurrection/vindication.

3. “They were right: He wouldn’t come down” is too clever after quoting “himself he cannot save.” They were not right about inability; Matthew 26:53 supports that He had power. The point is voluntary substitution, not that the mockers’ premise was right.

4. “The mockers were reciting a script they didn’t know existed” is loose. The Matthew 27:43 speakers include scribes/elders, so “didn’t know existed” strains credibility. Better: they did not know they were echoing it.

5. KJV words: no altered words found. Minor caution: punctuation/presentation is not exact in “they shake the head saying, He…” and the split quote “He saved others,” / “himself he cannot save.” If punctuation is part of your verbatim rule, revise.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Stop implying the same “crowd” performed both the head-wagging and Matthew 27:43 taunt.
2. Clarify “God didn’t” as “God did not take Him down from the cross.”
3. Replace “They were right” with wording that preserves His power and voluntary substitution.
