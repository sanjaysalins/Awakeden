# Independent review — gemini (OK, 39s)

The script has a compelling hook and strong core concept, successfully bridging the Davidic prophecy to the crucifixion without overstepping into unbiblical "fulfilment citation" territory. However, the execution of the landing contains a critical logic error that accidentally affirms a heresy, and there is a pronoun issue that muddles the clarity. 

Here are the specific findings:

**1. Logic / Doctrinal Error in the Landing (Accidental affirmation of impotence)**
- **Citation:** `"He saved others," they jeered, "himself he cannot save." They were right: He wouldn't come down…`
- **Critique:** The mockers specifically claim He *cannot* save Himself (impotence). By immediately following this with "They were right," you accidentally affirm their claim that Jesus lacked the power. The entire point of the gospel reframe (and your brief) is that they were WRONG about His inability ("cannot"), because His choice was voluntary ("would not"). It should be something like: *"They were wrong. It wasn't that He couldn't save Himself—it's that He wouldn't."*

**2. Pronoun Ambiguity / Confusion**
- **Citation:** `They dared God to rescue Him — and God didn't. Not because He lacked the power, but because rescuing Himself would have meant losing you.`
- **Critique:** This is grammatically disjointed on first hearing. The subject of the first sentence is "God" (the Father). In the second sentence, "He" and "Himself" suddenly shift to referring to Jesus. Structurally, it sounds like you are saying God the Father didn't rescue Jesus because *God* lacked the power, or because *God* rescuing Himself would lose you. The subjects need to be distinctly clarified (e.g., distinguishing the Father's restraint from the Son's restraint). 

**3. KJV Verbatim Violation (Missing Punctuation)**
- **Citation:** `"…they shake the head saying, He trusted on the LORD..."`
- **Critique:** The script is missing a comma. The exact KJV text of Psalm 22:7 is: `"they shake the head, saying,"`. The depth section's justification ("the comma after 'head' is presentation") is incorrect; the comma is present in the actual KJV text and must be included inside the quotation marks to pass a strict verbatim check.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix the logic error in the landing: do not say the mockers "were right" about "himself he cannot save." Contrast their "cannot" with His voluntary "would not."
2. Resolve the pronoun confusion in the "God didn't / He lacked / rescuing Himself" sequence so it's crystal clear who is doing what.
3. Restore the missing comma in the Psalm 22:7 quotation ("they shake the head, saying,").
