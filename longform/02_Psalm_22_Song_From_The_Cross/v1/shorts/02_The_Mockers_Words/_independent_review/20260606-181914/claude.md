# Independent review — claude (OK, 83s)

I verified all five quoted spans against the KJV cache (`data/kjv_cache.json`): Ps 22:7-8, Matt 27:43, 27:42, and "wagging their heads" (27:39) are all **exact KJV**. The citation-guard holds — the script claims "almost word for word… a script they didn't know existed," never a Matthew fulfilment-citation. Hook grips, CTA is grace-anchored, length is in range. Good craft. But there is one real problem at the most important line.

**Findings:**

1. **CLIMACTIC LINE UNDERCUTS ITS OWN DOCTRINE — "They were right."** The landing: *"'himself he cannot save.' They were right: He wouldn't come down…"* The entire short (and the brief's whole reason to exist) is **couldn't vs wouldn't** — voluntary substitution, not impotence. Affirming "**They were right**" *immediately after* quoting "himself he **cannot** save" reads, on first hearing, as conceding the "cannot." Then it silently swaps to "wouldn't come down" without flagging the reversal. A zero-Bible listener can easily hear: *the mockers were right that He couldn't.* That is the one heresy the script is built to avoid. The "right" should be pinned to the **outcome** (He indeed did not come down) while explicitly **denying the reason** (not couldn't — wouldn't). As written it concedes too much.

2. **"and God didn't" — slight trinitarian sloppiness.** *"They dared God to rescue Him — and God didn't."* Jesus is God; this is Father-not-rescuing-Son. Standard framing, but right after "God didn't" the listener needs to land on Christ's own willing choice ("He wouldn't"), and the agency shifts (God didn't → He was saving you). Tightening agency to Christ's volition throughout would strengthen the substitution point.

3. **Substitution stated twice — mild redundancy.** The conviction beat ("rescuing Himself would have meant losing you") and the landing ("because He was saving you") make the same move. Acceptable for emphasis, but if trimming for length, this is the slack.

4. **Length watch (~60s ceiling).** ~155–165 spoken words plus two Scripture reads with pre-quote pauses likely pushes 62–66s raw; the synth targets 59 and will compress — verify the two quote reads don't get rushed past reverence.

5. **Minor DEPTH-note inconsistency (not spoken).** The sourcing note says "the comma after 'head' is presentation," but neither the KJV cache ("they shake the head saying,") nor the spoken line has a comma after "head." Harmless, but fix the note so the audit trail is accurate.

Net: doctrinally sound, KJV-clean, honest guard — but the single most important line is ambiguous in exactly the dimension this short was created to protect. That warrants a revise before lock.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Recast "They were right: He wouldn't come down" so "right" attaches to the *outcome* and the script *explicitly denies* "cannot" (e.g. "They were wrong about why. He didn't come down — not because He couldn't, but because coming down meant leaving you"). Don't let the climax appear to endorse "himself he cannot save."
2. Tighten agency to Christ's own will across "and God didn't… He was saving you," so the voluntary-substitution thread reads cleanly on first hearing and avoids the Father/Son ambiguity.
3. Trim the doubled substitution statement (conviction vs landing) to recover length headroom and keep the ~59s target without rushing the two Scripture reads.
