# Independent review — gemini (OK, 39s)

The script excellently navigates the difficult theological waters of the dereliction, providing a clear, orthodox, and deeply moving presentation. It upholds the KJV text flawlessly while employing a very strong hook and landing. 

The insight that Jesus' faith remained intact because He still cried "my God" is a brilliant inclusion that guards against the "despair" error without slowing down the script. The substitutionary logic is clear, avoiding any heretical implications of a severed Trinity.

VERDICT: PASS
TOP FIXES:
1. None strictly required. The script flawlessly executes the brief and honors all doctrinal and textual constraints.
2. (Optional polish) "and with it the forsaking our sin deserved" is slightly clunky for the ear. Consider a minor tweak for audio flow, such as: "taking our place, and *bearing* the forsaking our sin deserved."
3. (Optional polish) "He didn't invent" is fine for a general audience, but to be strictly Christologically precise (since the Spirit of Christ inspired David to write the Psalm originally), you could say "He wasn't speaking them for the first time — He was quoting..." Again, not a blocker, just a nuance.
