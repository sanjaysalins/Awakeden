## Independent adversarial review — *The Forsaken Cry* (Psalm 22 short #3)

### What holds up

- **KJV (spoken clauses):** Psalm 22:1 — `"My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"` matches the cached KJV exactly. Matthew 27:46 — the English clause after Matthew’s *“that is to say”* is likewise exact KJV. The leading `…` is editorial clipping of Aramaic + gloss, not a word change inside the cited English.
- **Lost-faith guard:** `"This was no cry of lost faith — even forsaken, He still calls God 'my God.'"` is the right dereliction correction. Christ’s faith holds; He still addresses God as *my* God.
- **Penal substitution, not Trinity rupture:** `"the sinless Son taking our place, and with it the forsaking our sin deserved"` frames judicial forsakenness *borne*, not “God abandoned God.” No ontological severance is stated or implied.
- **Thread spine:** Hook (quotation) → David/Jesus juxtaposition → same cry → doctrinal guard → substitution → landing. No thread-swap.
- **Hook:** `"The most desolate words Jesus ever spoke, He didn't invent — He was quoting a song written a thousand years before the cross."` Grips fast; series thesis is audible.
- **Landing:** `"He was forsaken — so that you never will be"` does real substitution work (Heb 13:5 inversion), not a generic “will you trust Him?” stall. `"bring you home"` + `"Come to Him"` invite; no fear/gain-loss coercion.

### Findings (genuine residuals only)

**1. `"The very same words."` — accuracy overclaim (minor, but real under ACCURACY #1)**  
After two identical KJV English lines, this line asserts phonetic identity. Matthew records the cross-cry as Aramaic (`Eli, Eli, lama sabachthani?`) and then glosses it. Psalm 22:1 is Hebrew. The link is textual/theological — Matthew’s *“that is to say”* — not literally the same spoken words in the same language. For a series beat built on “Jesus deliberately QUOTED it,” the script never signals *deliberate psalm citation* beyond the hook’s `"He was quoting"`; `"The very same words"` could mislead a careful listener into thinking Jesus spoke the KJV English (or even the Hebrew) verbatim on the cross. Not heresy, but a precision slip on the series’ strongest accuracy claim. Safer: *“the same cry”* / *“the psalm’s opening line”* / *“what David wrote, Jesus cried.”*

**2. `"with it the forsaking our sin deserved"` — first-hearing compression at the doctrinal hinge**  
This is the penal-substitution payload in ~60s. Two problems in one phrase:
- **Grammar:** Missing *that* — *“the forsaking **that** our sin deserved”* — makes the line audibly clipped.
- **Logic:** *“with it”* is elliptical. A zero-Bible listener must infer: with taking our place → comes the forsakenness our sin earned. That inference is doable but not clean on one pass, especially right after the faith-intact guard. The dereliction doctrine is orthodox; the **delivery** is the weak point.

**3. `"He was forsaken — so that you never will be."` — borderline gain cadence (brief-defended, still audible)**  
The project brief explicitly approves this as substitution-promise, not Isaiah-style *“so you would never have to.”* Fair. But on a cold listen, the second-person *“so that you never will be”* still scans as personal payoff before *“bring you home”* lands the grace frame. Not a FAIL under your stated guards; worth noting as residual ear-risk, not a blocker.

**4. `"Jesus made it His own"` — low-grade clarity fuzz**  
After *“David wrote it,”* *“made it His own”* could sound like authorship/rewrite rather than messianic assumption of the psalm. The quotes immediately clarify, so this is polish, not a structural failure.

**5. `"at the ninth hour"` — unexplained for zero-Bible listeners**  
Minor. Most will still follow; some will not know it means ~3 p.m. Not doctrinal.

### What I did **not** flag (and why)

- **KJV ellipsis in Matt 27:46:** Typographic clip of non-English prefix; spoken English is exact. Not an alteration inside the cited clause.
- **No spoken Trinity-severance negation:** Substitution-bearing language is sufficient for a 60s short; explicit anti–“God turned His back” apologetics isn’t required here.
- **No whole-psalm incipit claim:** Correctly avoided per long-form panel.
- **“Come to Him”:** Generic, but preceded by a specific substitution inversion; acceptable CTA.

Doctrinally orthodox on dereliction (faith intact, penal substitution, grace landing). KJV spoken quotes are exact. No invented doctrine, no despair heresy, no Trinity-severance claim. Residuals are **precision and audio clarity at the hinge**, not structural failure.
