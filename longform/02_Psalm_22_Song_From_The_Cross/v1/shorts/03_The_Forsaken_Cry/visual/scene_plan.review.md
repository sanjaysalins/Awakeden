## Visual reading
The arc opens on Christ crying out from the cross, then reaches back to David writing the forsaken-man psalm. The proof movement is the ninth-hour darkness and the lifted cry 'My God, my God'. The conviction holds on the face that still calls God 'my God' even forsaken, and the substitution — the sinless Son bearing the forsaking our sin deserved. The landing turns toward a break of pale light behind the dark cross: forsaken so you never will be, the way home opened from the dark. The hero close is the crucified Christ against that breaking light.

## Writer's own red-team notes
Jaded Viewer risk: a generic 'sad Christ on a cross' could read as any passion image — so the cry is anchored to the SPECIFIC dereliction (head lifted, mouth open in the quoted cry) and the ninth-hour darkness, and the conviction face shows trust-amid-darkness (still 'my God'), not despair. The substitution scene (9) must NOT sever the Trinity — rendered as darkness/sin borne on Him, not the Father turning into a separate figure. Two scroll/David scenes (2, 12) kept distinct (writing vs the thousand-years diptych). Hero is the cross with breaking light (gospel-pivot), not the most anguished face.
