<speaker name="narrator">The most desolate words Jesus ever spoke, He didn't invent — He was quoting a song written a thousand years before the cross. Psalm twenty-two opens in the voice of a forsaken man. David wrote it; Jesus made it His own. David's very first line: "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" A thousand years later, at the ninth hour, Jesus cried it from the cross: "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?" The very same words. This was no cry of lost faith — even forsaken, He still calls God "my God." It was the sinless Son taking our place, and bearing the forsaking that our sin deserved. He was forsaken — so that you never will be. He went into that dark to bring you home, to a Father who will never let you go. That's Jesus. Come to Him.</speaker>
