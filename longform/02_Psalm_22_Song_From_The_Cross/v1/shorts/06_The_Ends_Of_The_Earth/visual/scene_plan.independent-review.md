# Scene plan — Independent red-team audit

**Verdict:** `LOCKED`  ·  **Failed gates:** 0

## Visual reading
The arc opens on one forsaken man on a lone cross in an immense emptiness, then the psalm's last lines reach outward and the cross on its hill is shown against a whole world horizon. The hero is the cross radiating light to the far edges of the land — the reach the song foresaw. From the tiny hill in one corner of the empire, the gospel goes out by road and sea; the ancient promise to Abraham (all nations blessed) and the light to the Gentiles carry the OT echoes; kindreds of many lands gather in worship and pilgrims turn nation after nation. It lands on a single small figure on a far road turning toward the light, an open way left for whoever will come, and the cross cresting the whole earth at dawn.

## Writer's own red-team notes
Jaded Viewer risks: (1) 'the nations' can become a repetitive parade of bowing crowds — I varied it across gathered worshippers (8), a pilgrim procession (11), a distant people first catching light (9), and a single lone turner (12), each a distinct framing and idea. (2) 'light to the nations' invites the banned heaven-opens beam — I used a low-horizon dawn reaching across dark ground, never a vertical god-beam. (3) The global theme could float free of the cross — every wide scene keeps the cross or its light as the source. (4) Avoided modern globe/map anachronisms; the 'world' is rendered as far lands, seas and horizons.

## Scenes

  · purpose: open on the lone forsaken man dying alone — the contrast the hook turns on
  · rationale: isolation rather than a crowd; the worldwide theme begins from one man

- **2. The Song Opens Its Arms** ★ `[single/close/theological-centre]`

  · purpose: the smallness of the place against the vastness of the world

- **6. The Gospel Goes Out** ★ `[unified/mid/nt-gospel-link]`
  · purpose: the gospel's going-out to the nations after the cross and empty tomb

- **7. In Thee All Nations Blessed** `[unified/wide/ot-echo]`

- **9. A Light to the Gentiles** ★ `[single/mid/ot-echo]`
  · purpose: light reaching a people in darkness (Isaiah 49:6 -> Acts 13:47)
  · rationale: low-horizon dawn reaching across dark ground, never a vertical god-beam

- **10. The Empty Tomb** `[single/mid/revelation]`
  · purpose: the One who died and rose — the gospel's engine

- **11. Nation After Nation** `[single/wide/symbolic-support]`
  · purpose: peoples turning age after age
  · rationale: a pilgrim procession rather than another bowing crowd
  · visible: a long procession of pilgrims of many lands on a road toward distant light
- **12. Wherever You Are** `[single/mid/personal-confrontation]`
  · purpose: the landing's turn to the single listener
  · rationale: one small figure turning, the global made personal
  · visible: a lone small figure on a far road turned toward distant light
- **13. Room to Turn** `[single/mid/human-response]`
  · purpose: the open way left for whoever will come
  · rationale: an open road to the cross rather than a literal altar call
  · visible: a worn open road leading to a cross on a low rise at dawn
- **14. The Whole Earth at Dawn** ★ `[single/wide/closing-devotional]`
  · purpose: close on the cross over the whole earth at dawn
  · rationale: the song that swept the earth, lands bright beneath the cross
