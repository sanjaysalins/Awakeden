**Verdict:** `LOCKED`  ·  **Failed gates:** 0

## Visual reading
The arc opens on one forsaken man on a lone cross in an immense emptiness, then the psalm's last lines reach outward and the cross on its hill is shown against a whole world horizon. The hero is the cross radiating light to the far edges of the land — the reach the song foresaw. From the tiny hill in one corner of the empire, the gospel goes out by road and sea; the ancient promise to Abraham (all nations blessed) and the light to the Gentiles carry the OT echoes; kindreds of many lands gather in worship and pilgrims turn nation after nation. It lands on a single small figure on a far road turning toward the light, an open way left for whoever will come, and the cross cresting the whole earth at dawn.

## Writer's own red-team notes
Jaded Viewer risks: (1) 'the nations' can become a repetitive parade of bowing crowds — I varied it across gathered worshippers (8), a pilgrim procession (11), a distant people first catching light (9), and a single lone turner (12), each a distinct framing and idea. (2) 'light to the nations' invites the banned heaven-opens beam — I used a low-horizon dawn reaching across dark ground, never a vertical god-beam. (3) The global theme could float free of the cross — every wide scene keeps the cross or its light as the source. (4) Avoided modern globe/map anachronisms; the 'world' is rendered as far lands, seas and horizons.
