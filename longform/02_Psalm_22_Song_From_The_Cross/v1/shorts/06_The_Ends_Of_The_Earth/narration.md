# The Ends of the Earth — Psalm 22 short #6  (distilled from the Psalm 22 long)

- **Series:** `jesus-in-ot` (Awakeden) — short. Psalm 22 short-series, beat #6 (🟡 strong — the psalm's own prophecy + history).
- **Backed by (the deep study):** `longform/02_Psalm_22_Song_From_The_Cross` — the locked Psalm 22 long-form.
- **Beat distilled:** the GLOBAL turn. The psalm of the cross opens in one forsaken man's agony and ENDS with
  "all the ends of the world" turning to God (Ps 22:27). The dying man's song foresaw a worldwide turning — and
  the gospel did spread to the nations.
- **ACCURACY GUARD (why 🟡):** This is the psalm's OWN prophetic statement (v27 directly says the nations will
  turn) + the HISTORICAL fact of the gospel's global spread — NOT a specific NT verse-citation of Ps 22:27. The
  short frames it as "the song foresaw it; then it happened," tied thematically to the Great Commission (Matt
  28:19) and the nations before the Lamb (Rev 7:9 — kept in the ledger, not overclaimed). "Turn unto the LORD"
  (Yahweh) applied to worshipping the crucified-risen Christ is sound (Christ is worshipped — Rev 5; Phil 2:10-11).
  NOT universalism: people FROM every nation, offered to those who come ("Come to Him").
- **Process:** ONE red-team + ONE 5-CLI panel → LOCK (the standard lean review).
- **Format:** 9:16, ~60s, Gospel Five-Beat, CTA-to-Jesus.
- **Status:** v2 — **LOCKED** (2026-06-06). Lean process: ONE red-team (LOCK — all 3 guards honored) + ONE

**[narrator]**

## DEPTH & SOURCING  *(not spoken)*
- **KJV verbatim** (self-verified via `pipeline/scripture.fetch_kjv`, 2026-06-06): Ps 22:27 — "All the ends of
  the world shall remember and turn unto the LORD: and all the kindreds of the nations shall worship before
  thee." — exact, full verse. (Only one quoted span.)
- **Prophecy + history, not a single NT citation:** v27 is the psalm's OWN forward statement that the nations
  will turn; the fulfilment is the HISTORICAL global spread of the gospel — so the short says "his song says…
  then it happened," not "this verse was fulfilled in [chapter]." Thematically anchored to the Great Commission
  (Matt 28:19, "teach all nations") and Rev 7:9 (a multitude of all nations before the Lamb) — kept here, not
  overclaimed in the spoken text.
- **"Turn unto the LORD" → worshipping the crucified-risen Christ:** sound — turning to Yahweh is, in the NT,
  turning to the Lord Jesus (Phil 2:10-11; Rev 5 worships the Lamb). The short ties the nations' worship to
  "the very One who had died" without conflating the persons or claiming the psalm names Jesus.
- **NOT universalism:** "nation after nation turned" = people FROM the nations (Rev 7:9), not every individual;
  the landing offers it personally ("Come to Him"), not a declaration that all are saved.
- **Grace landing, does new work:** the one-forsaken-man → whole-world arc, then the personal turn ("the song
  was reaching all the way to you"). "Turn"/"ends of the world" echo the verse. No fear/gain-loss. CTA-to-Jesus.
