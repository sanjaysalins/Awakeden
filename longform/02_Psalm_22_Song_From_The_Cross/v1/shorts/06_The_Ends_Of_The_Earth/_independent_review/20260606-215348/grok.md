**KJV verbatim**: The single quoted block is exact KJV Psalm 22:27 with no altered words inside the quotation marks. Attribution is explicit. No violations here.

**Over-read of the historical claim (core guard violation)**:

Exact line: "A man dying in one corner of the Roman Empire — and his song says the ends of the earth will turn to God. It sounded impossible. Then it happened. From that cross the message went out, and nation after nation turned to God..."

The artifact's own accuracy guard states this must be "the psalm's OWN prophetic statement (v27 directly says the nations will turn) + the HISTORICAL fact of the gospel's global spread — NOT a specific NT verse-citation" and frames it as "'the song foresaw it; then it happened,'" while insisting on "nation after nation" not "literally every nation."

The spoken text does the "then it happened" move and immediately follows with "From that cross the message went out." This presents the psalm's "shall remember and turn unto the LORD" (full global scope language) as having been realized by the historical spread. "Nation after nation" arrives as a qualifier after the absolute claim. The psalm's vision ("all the ends of the world") is eschatological in scope in standard Christian reading; the short collapses the prophecy into a past-tense historical event without spoken qualification. This is the exact over-read the brief instructs to watch for.

**Person-conflation / identification risk (second guard violation)**:

Exact line: "...nation after nation turned to God, worshipping the very One who had died."

The quoted psalm addresses "the LORD" and "thee." The narration immediately glosses the fulfillment as worship directed to "the very One who had died." The depth section correctly notes that NT worship (Phil 2:10-11, Rev 5) applies "Lord" language to the risen Christ and that turning to Yahweh is fulfilled in turning to Christ. However, in the spoken text alone there is no buffer: no "the gospel reveals," no "the message called them to," no "as Lord." The equation is made directly by the narrator. The guard explicitly says the psalm is "never said to NAME Jesus" and warns against person-conflation. This sentence is the precise point where that line is thinnest in the audio.

**Imported language that weakens the "psalm's own prophecy" claim**:

Exact line: "It was never a local tragedy — it was for the nations, every kindred and tongue."

"every kindred and tongue" is not Psalm 22 language. Psalm 22:27 says "all the kindreds of the nations." The "every kindred and tongue" formula is Revelation 5:9 / 7:9 language. The accuracy guard stresses that this is the psalm's OWN forward statement in v27 plus historical spread, not a blended apocalyptic vision. Using the Revelation phrasing to describe what the psalm envisioned blurs the stated methodological guard.

**Secondary clarity / hook issue (first-hearing test)**:

Hook: "The psalm Jesus cried from the cross begins in agony — one man, forsaken, dying. But look how it ends: with the whole world."

For a listener with zero Bible knowledge, "with the whole world" is too abstract to grip in the first ~5 seconds. It sets up a contrast but does not yet deliver what the prophecy actually says (nations remembering and turning to worship). The meaning only clarifies once the quote arrives. The next line ("the song throws its arms open to every nation") helps, but the opening hook payload remains impressionistic rather than clear.
