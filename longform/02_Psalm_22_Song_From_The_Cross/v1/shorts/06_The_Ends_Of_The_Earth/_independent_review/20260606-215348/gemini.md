# Independent review — gemini (OK, 23s)

This is a remarkably clean, well-constructed script. The transitions are seamless, the contrasts are stark ("one man, forsaken" vs "every nation"), and the historical grounding ("one corner of the Roman Empire") makes the prophetic claim tangible. The guards provided in the brief are perfectly executed.

A few specific notes on why it succeeds:
- **No Conflation/Sleight of Hand:** "nation after nation turned to God, worshipping the very One who had died" beautifully bridges the Psalm's reference to Yahweh with the historical reality of the Great Commission and the worship of Christ, remaining entirely orthodox without forcing the text of the Psalm to say something it doesn't. 
- **Not Universalism:** The landing ("includes wherever you are... reaching all the way to you. Come to Him.") expertly reframes the global scope into a personal invitation, effectively neutralizing any universalist misread of "the whole world."
- **Strong Hook:** The opening instantly establishes the "narrow to wide" spine that drives the entire script.
