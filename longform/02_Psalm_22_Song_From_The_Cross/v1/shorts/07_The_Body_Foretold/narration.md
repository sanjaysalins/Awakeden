  the stretch of a man hung by his arms) and "I may tell all my bones: they look and stare upon me" (v17, ribs

## DEPTH & SOURCING  *(not spoken)*
- **KJV verbatim** (self-verified via `pipeline/scripture.fetch_kjv`, 2026-06-06):
  - Ps 22:14: "I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint…" — exact words; trailing ellipsis
    clips "my heart is like wax; it is melted in the midst of my bowels." (deliberately dropped — see guard).
  - Ps 22:17: "I may tell all my bones: they look and stare upon me." — exact, full verse.
- **Descriptive match, not a cited prophecy:** no NT verse cites Ps 22:14/17; the short claims the psalm
  PICTURES the crucified body (suspension dislocates joints; the exposed, countable ribs; the staring crowd) —
  framed as "here it is… written early," NOT "fulfilled in [chapter]." Predictive precision: David ~1000 BC;
  Roman crucifixion is centuries later ("Rome would not make it their instrument for centuries" — avoids the
  false "Rome invented it").
- **NO medical over-claim:** v14's "heart like wax… melted" (the popular cardiac-rupture proof-text) is OMITTED;
  "out of joint" is the only physiological detail kept, and it's described plainly ("body hung by its arms…
  body hangs when it's suspended by the arms"), not as clinical proof. "I may tell all my bones" → "count every
  bone… stared at him" (tracks the verse: all the bones, and they stare at HIM — not narrowed to ribs/crowd).
- **Grace landing (no transaction):** "what He bore to bring you home… He endured all of it, willingly, for
  you" — bearing language, voluntary ("willingly"), no "cost/paid/debt" cadence, no fear/gain-loss. CTA-to-Jesus.
- **Distinct from short #1** (garments): that one = the clothes gambled (Ps 22:18 + John 19:24); this one = the
  body itself (Ps 22:14, 17). No overlap of quoted spans.
