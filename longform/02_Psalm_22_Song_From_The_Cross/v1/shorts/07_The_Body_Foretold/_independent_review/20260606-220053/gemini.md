# Independent review — gemini (OK, 30s)

The script is remarkably tight, disciplined, and strictly adheres to the guardrails. It avoids the cardiac-rupture trap, handles the KJV translation of "tell" perfectly, and lands with a beautiful, grace-anchored CTA ("every wrenched joint, every cold stare"). 

However, there are two minor structural/phrasing issues that slightly blunt the momentum before the landing:

1. **Redundant Timeline / Double "Rome" Drop:**
In the hook, you establish the timeline beautifully: *"…a thousand years before Rome ever raised a cross."* 
But then, right before the climax, you repeat the exact same concept: *"Rome would not make it their instrument for centuries."* 
This repetition stalls the pacing. Since the hook already did the heavy lifting regarding the timeline, you can cut the second "Rome" line entirely to make the turn sharper. 
*Example fix:* "David never saw a crucifixion. Yet here it is…"

2. **Tangled Metaphor:**
*"Yet here it is — the cross, in a body, written early."*
"The cross, in a body" is a clunky pile-up of concepts that doesn't quite parse on first hearing. Does it mean the cross is *inside* a body? It interrupts the emotional flow right before the landing. Simplify it to focus on the physical agony or the reality of the event.
*Example fix:* "Yet here it is — the physical agony of the cross, written early." OR "Yet here it is — the cross, mapped onto a human body, centuries early."

VERDICT: PASS
TOP FIXES:
1. Cut the redundant "Rome would not make it their instrument for centuries" line; the hook already established this timeline perfectly.
2. Smooth out the clunky phrase "the cross, in a body" (e.g., "the physical agony of the cross, written early").
