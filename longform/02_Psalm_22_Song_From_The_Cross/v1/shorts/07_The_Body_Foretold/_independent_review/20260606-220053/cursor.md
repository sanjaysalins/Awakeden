### Genuine residual issues

**1. First-hearing subject fracture (clarity — moderate, real)**

The script never bridges first-person psalm voice to third-person gloss the way locked short #1 does (“David wrote first person… describing someone else”).

Sequence a cold listener hears:
- “Hear how it pictures **the dying man**”
- KJV first person: “**I** am poured out… **my** bones…”
- Gloss switches: “**He** could count **his** own ribs…”
- Much later: “**David** never saw a crucifixion”
- Landing: “**He** bore… for you”

Who is “He” after the quote? David? Jesus? A generic sufferer? Short #1 solved this explicitly; this short assumes series memory. For a zero-Bible listener (your clarity lens), the proof beat is logically muddy for ~15 seconds. That is not a nit — it is a followability failure in the middle.

**Exact phrases:** `"Hear how it pictures the dying man"` → `"I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint"` → `"He could count his own ribs"` — the pronoun snap with no bridge.

---

**2. Orphan quoted material — “poured out like water” (clarity + accuracy alignment — minor but genuine)**

The body thesis is v14’s **“out of joint”** + v17’s **“tell all my bones / stare.”** Yet the v14 quote opens with **“I am poured out like water,”** which is never glossed and is not part of the defended beat (you dropped the wax-heart medical line; this is a different bodily image).

A listener who does not know the psalm gets an opaque image, then a pivot to joints. In a 60s short, that is dead auditory weight and slightly dilutes the “body foretold” precision you are guarding.

**Exact phrase:** `"I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint…"` — first clause buys nothing for the thesis.

---

**3. “Jesus quoted as He died” sits directly above different verses (clarity — minor mislead risk)**

**“Psalm twenty-two — the song Jesus quoted as He died.”** is immediately followed by v14/v17, not v1 (“My God, my God…”). Jesus quoted the *opening* of the psalm on the cross; He did not speak these lines aloud there.

A casual listener can easily hear this as: *these are the words Jesus said while dying.* That is not what you intend, and it is avoidable with one clarifying word (“same psalm,” “other lines from it,” etc.).

**Exact phrase:** `"the song Jesus quoted as He died. Hear how it pictures the dying man"`

---

**4. “Ribs” / “stretched bare” — gloss narrows the text (accuracy tone — low, within your brief but worth flagging)**

**“He could count his own ribs, stretched bare”** is an authorized gloss, but it is not what v17 says. KJV: **“I may tell all my bones”** — *tell* = count/recount is defensible; narrowing to **ribs** is crucifixion-shaped inference, not lexical necessity. **“Stretched bare”** imports exposure/nudity the verse does not state (staring crowd, yes; stripped bare, not in v17).

This does not break your guards, but under **ACCURACY #1** and adversarial default, it is the one place the spoken script outruns the text slightly. If challenged, the honest answer is “a common crucifixion read,” not “what the verse says.”

**Exact phrases:** `"He could count his own ribs, stretched bare"`

---

**5. Suspension gloss stated as forensic certainty (medical/descriptive-match tone — low)**

**“Out of joint — just what happens to a body hung by its arms, its own weight dragging the bones apart.”**

Your guard allows plain suspension description and drops cardiac-rupture speculation — good. But **“just what happens”** + **“dragging the bones apart”** presents a biomechanical mechanism as settled fact, while the beat is explicitly 🟡 *descriptive match*, not 🟢 airtight prophecy. Poetic “out of joint” can carry broader agony sense in Hebrew laments; the script does not hedge at all.

This is weaker than the dropped wax-heart over-claim, and in-engine already accepted it — but it is the only remaining place the narration sounds like medical proof-text rather than “one way this line reads on a cross.”

**Exact phrase:** `"just what happens to a body hung by its arms, its own weight dragging the bones apart"`
