**Genuine residual issues:**

1. **The dislocation mechanism is asserted as settled fact — this is the same over-claim category the brief tried to escape.** The line *"Out of joint — just what happens to a body hung by its arms, its own weight dragging the bones apart"* states a forensic mechanism with certainty ("just what happens," "dragging the bones apart"). Dropping the cardiac-rupture theory but then asserting a confident joint-dislocation mechanism re-imports the same risk in milder form. Hebrew "out of joint" (separated/loosed) is being read as anatomical dislocation — that's an interpretation, not a given. Soften to descriptive-possibility ("the kind of thing that happens to a body hung by its arms") so it stays a *picture*, not a clinical claim.

2. **Hook attributes authorial intent that the script later contradicts.** *"A king once wrote down, in unbearable detail, what crucifixion does to a human body"* implies David consciously documented crucifixion — but the middle correctly says *"David never saw a crucifixion."* The descriptive-match thesis is "the psalm *pictures* it," not "David *wrote down what crucifixion does.*" The hook overstates intent; it's resolved later, but the opening sentence as written is stronger than the honest frame. Minor reshape (e.g., "a king wrote words that read like an autopsy of the cross") keeps the grip without the intent overclaim.

3. **Doc-ledger error (not spoken, but fix it):** the DEPTH note says *"No 'pierced' here (that's short #1)."* Short #1 is the garments (Ps 22:18, v note's own header). "Pierced" is Ps 22:16 — a different beat, not short #1. The parenthetical is wrong and could misroute the series tracking.

**Non-issues (confirmed sound):** "the song Jesus quoted as He died" (Matt 27:46 — accurate); "Rome would not make it their instrument for centuries" correctly dodges the false "Rome invented crucifixion"; landing is bearing/voluntary language ("bore… willingly… for you / Come to Him") with no transaction, fear, or gain-loss; one thread spine (body-described-early → two verses → He bore it for you) holds; distinct from short #1 with no quoted-span overlap.

The piece is close and the guards mostly held. The one substantive content risk is #1 — a confident physiological mechanism is still a physiological over-claim, just a more plausible one than the heart-wax theory. Worth softening before LOCK.
