# Scene plan — Self-review panel

**Verdict:** `LOCKED`  ·  **Failed gates:** 0

## Visual reading
The arc opens on the Psalm's final illuminated line and travels a thousand years to the cross's finished word, holding the two as bookends of one redemption joined only by light — never collapsing the Hebrew 'done' and the Greek 'finished' into a verbal pun, but letting both rest on the same note of a completed saving work. From the scroll and the scribe telling it forward to a people not yet born, to the bowed head and the torn veil, to the day's end and the hands at rest, the eye moves toward a single gospel pivot: Christ on the cross saying it is done. It lands on the released burden, the open lit door, and the cross at dawn over the road home — nothing left to finish, only Someone to come home to.

## Writer's own red-team notes
Jaded Viewer risks: (1) an abstract 'finished work' theme can drift into floating-text slideshow — anchored every beat in a concrete tableau (frayed scroll thread, torn veil threads, set-down burden, lit doorway). (2) The resonance could be mis-read as a Hebrew/Greek pun — the diptych keeps the two scripts visibly DIFFERENT, joined only by a band of light, never overlaid. (3) Too many cross close-ups would feel repetitive — varied them (low-angle whole, close bowed head, distant silhouette trio, dawn-and-road) and spread non-cross anchors between them. Revision: removed the banned token 'frame' from scenes 1/3/5 subject_blocks (SP-G5).

## Scenes
- **1. The Psalm's Last Line** ★ `[single/close/opening-hook]`
  · purpose: open the arc on the thread itself — the psalm's closing line caught in light
  · rationale: the hook is the anchor object, not a generic glowing book; the whole composition is weighted to one illuminated closing line
  · visible: an ancient Hebrew scroll, its final lower line lit while the upper lines sit in shadow, frayed thread on the wooden stave
- **2. A People Not Yet Born** `[unified/mid/ot-echo]`
  · purpose: carry Ps 22:31's forward look — the saving work told to generations not yet born
  · rationale: uses the verse's own clause rather than a generic prophet at a desk
  · visible: an aged scribe over the open psalm scroll by lamplight, dim background vignettes of future generations
- **3. The Fingertip on the Final Word** ★ `[single/close/theological-centre]`
  · purpose: rest on the psalm's last clause as it is quoted
  · rationale: a macro anchor for the quoted line, avoiding a wide reading-room cliche
  · visible: a weathered fingertip beneath the last clause of Hebrew script, cracked dark ink, lamplit parchment
- **4. It Is Finished** ★ `[single/low-angle/nt-gospel-link/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: the gospel pivot — the finished saving work on the cross, the One who said it is done
  · rationale: the CTA's destination; designed iconic, near-still and reverent to bookend the cut, not the most emotional moment
  · visible: the crucified Christ from a low angle, head bowed, robed at the loins, dark sky, a faint light behind the lowered head
- **5. The Greek Word** ★ `[single/close/theological-centre]`
  · purpose: John's finished word on the page — a thousand years and a different tongue from the psalm
  · rationale: shows the visibly DIFFERENT script, guarding the resonance-not-citation truth
  · visible: an early Greek codex page, a line of Greek letters, a fingertip beneath, raking lamplight
- **6. A Thousand Years Apart** ★ `[unified/wide/ot-echo/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: hold scroll and cross as two bookends of one redemption, joined only by light
  · rationale: states the resonance as composition — never an overlay or a pun
  · visible: the scribe over his scroll at left, a distant cross on a dark hill at right, a vertical band of pale light between
- **7. The Bowed Head** `[single/close/revelation/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: the peace of completion in the lowered face
  · rationale: a close read of the finished moment without repeating the whole-cross framing
  · visible: the crucified Christ's lowered head and shoulder, crown of thorns, faint light along the brow
- **8. The Torn Veil** ★ `[single/mid/nt-gospel-link]`
  · purpose: the finished work opens the way — the temple veil rent (Matt 27:51)
  · rationale: a real NT consequence of the finished work, not an invented symbol
  · visible: the temple veil rent top to bottom, golden light through the tear, dust caught in the shaft
- **9. The Day Is Done** `[single/wide/symbolic-support]`
  · purpose: the held quiet of a day, and a work, brought to its end
  · rationale: a breathing wide beat that says 'it is done' without another face
  · visible: three bare crosses in silhouette on a dark hill, a low red sun at the horizon
- **10. Hands at Rest** `[single/close/emotional-turning/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: labour ended — the pierced hands utterly still
  · rationale: the finished work read in the body, not in text
  · visible: two open pierced palms at rest, nail-marks dark in the centres, garment edge beneath
- **11. The Finished Work** ★ `[unified/mid/theological-centre/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: the central cross holding everything the finished work secures
  · rationale: the theological centre as one coherent image with half-dissolved echoes, not a montage
  · visible: the central cross with the robed figure, dim vignettes of scroll, torn veil, open door, generations
- **12. Nothing Left to Carry** `[single/mid/personal-confrontation]`
  · purpose: the conviction — the weight laid down, nothing left to finish
  · rationale: embodies 'nothing left for you' without a debt/transaction framing
  · visible: a kneeling figure at the foot of a rough cross, a heavy burden set down beside loosened hands
- **13. The Open Door Home** ★ `[single/mid/human-response]`
  · purpose: the landing's invitation — Someone to come home to
  · rationale: an open-home image rather than a literal altar-call cliche
  · visible: a humble doorway standing open, warm lamplight across the threshold, a worn path, an olive tree
- **14. The One Who Said It Is Done** `[unified/mid/closing-devotional/jesus=passion]`
  · purpose: close on the finished work and its open welcome
  · rationale: lands the cut on the cross at dawn over the road home, echoing the hero
  · visible: the cross at dawn, warm gold light, a worn road leading to an open lit door
