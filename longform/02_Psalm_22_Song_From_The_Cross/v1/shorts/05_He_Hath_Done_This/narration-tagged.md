<speaker name="narrator">The last line of Psalm twenty-two, and the last word Jesus spoke in John's gospel, both land on a finished work — a thousand years apart. The psalm closes looking ahead — to God's saving work, accomplished, and told to a people not yet born. Its final line: "…that he hath done this." Done. And as Jesus hung dying, John records His final word: "It is finished". Different words, in different tongues, a thousand years apart — but the same note: a finished work. So what, exactly, was finished? Everything it would take to bring you home to God — the whole work, complete. Nothing left for you to earn, nothing left to prove, nothing to add. It is done. Come to Him.</speaker>
