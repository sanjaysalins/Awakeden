## DEPTH & SOURCING  *(not spoken)*
- **KJV verbatim** (self-verified via `pipeline/scripture.fetch_kjv`, 2026-06-06):
  - Ps 22:31: "…that he hath done this." — exact; leading ellipsis clips "They shall come, and shall declare his
    righteousness unto a people that shall be born," — the quoted clause is the verse's final words.
  - John 19:30: "It is finished" — exact WORDS; KJV has a colon after ("…It is finished: and he bowed his
    head…"), so the spoken line places the terminal period OUTSIDE the quote — no non-KJV punctuation inside.
- **RESONANCE, not citation (the load-bearing guard):** Ps 22:31 Hebrew *ʿāśâ* ("done/accomplished") ≠ John 19:30
  Greek *tetelestai* ("finished/accomplished"). They are DIFFERENT words; John does not cite Psalm 22 for
  this. The short states this explicitly ("different words, in different tongues… but the same note") so it claims
  only a thematic echo of a COMPLETED saving work — never a word-for-word prophecy or a Greek/Hebrew pun.
- **No word-count claim:** "he hath done this" is FOUR words (the long-form's earlier "three words" was a
  flagged error); the short avoids counting and lets the clause speak.
- **Context of Ps 22:31:** "he hath done this" caps the psalm's turn to worldwide praise (vv27-31) — God's
  righteous saving work, declared to future generations. The thematic link to "It is finished" (the finished
  work of redemption) is faithful as resonance.
- **Grace landing (no transaction framing):** "the whole work of bringing you home — complete. Nothing left for
  you to earn… He has done it." NO "paid in full"/debt cadence (removed in the Isaiah lock); grace, not works.
  CTA-to-Jesus. Does new work (the two-bookends-of-the-cross resonance).
