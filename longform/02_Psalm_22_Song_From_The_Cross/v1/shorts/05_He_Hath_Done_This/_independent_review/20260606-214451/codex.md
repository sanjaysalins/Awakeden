# Independent review — codex (OK, 60s)

Findings:

1. **KJV punctuation problem in the artifact notes.** The spoken line is handled correctly as `"It is finished".`, but the support section says `John 19:30: "It is finished." — exact`. That is not exact KJV punctuation; KJV has `It is finished:`. Also `("It is finished," John 19:30)` puts a comma inside the quote.

2. **“Last word Jesus spoke on the cross” is too absolute.** The phrases `the last word Jesus spoke on the cross` and `He breathed one last word:` overclaim beyond John’s account. John records this as Jesus’ final saying before bowing His head, but Luke 23:46 records another saying at death. This should be scoped to John.

3. **Psalm quote ellipsis weakens the “KJV exact” claim.** `"…that he hath done this."` preserves the words, but the ellipsis is not KJV text. If the project is policing punctuation strictly, this should not be called “exact” without qualification.

No genuine verbal/etymological fulfilment overclaim found in the spoken narration; the `Different words, in different tongues... but the same note` guard does its job. No debt-transaction landing found.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Scope “last word” to John’s Gospel, not all cross sayings.
2. Remove non-KJV punctuation inside `"It is finished"` in metadata/source notes.
3. Treat the Psalm 22 ellipsis as an omission marker, not part of an “exact” KJV quote.
