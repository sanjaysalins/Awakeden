# Independent review — grok (OK, 76s)

**Findings (adversarial read, zero-Bible-knowledge listener + accuracy guard in view):**

**1. Direct violation of the stated KJV-punctuation guard on the load-bearing John quote.**

Spoken line:
```
**[narrator — KJV, John 19:30]**
"It is finished".
```

The period is inside/attached to the quoted material. The artifact brief itself is explicit: "John 19:30: KJV has 'It is finished:' (colon, verse continues 'and he bowed his head') so the period is placed OUTSIDE the quote (no non-KJV punctuation inside)." The DEPTH & SOURCING section repeats the same error by writing "It is finished." as the "exact" form. This is not a style nit; it is the exact problem the brief told the final reviewer to scrutinize hardest. The Psalm quote uses ellipsis + period acceptably; the John quote does not match the rule given for this short.

**2. "Carried the cross all the way to the end" creates immediate clarity failure and muddies the "finished" claim.**

Exact line:
```
Done. And when Jesus had carried the cross all the way to the end, He breathed one last word:
```

A listener who knows the surface story (Jesus physically carrying the cross, Simon of Cyrene stepping in) will hear this as a reference to the walk to Golgotha, not the completion of the redemptive work. The "Done." echo from Psalm 22:31 is supposed to land on the accomplishment, but this sentence blurs it into the physical journey. Zero-knowledge listener cannot reliably track what "finished" now refers to. This is a first-hearing failure on the central thesis.

**3. Claim-then-qualify structure undercuts the "thematic resonance only, not verbal fulfilment" guard.**

Hook / early lines:
```
The last line of Psalm twenty-two, and the last word Jesus spoke on the cross, land on the very same truth — a thousand years apart.

David ends the song looking ahead — to God's saving work, accomplished, and told to a people not yet born.
```

Guard sentence (comes later):
```
Different words, in different tongues, a thousand years apart — but the same note: a finished work.
```

The strong, unqualified assertions ("very same truth", "David ends the song looking ahead — to God's saving work, accomplished") arrive first. The explicit disambiguation ("different words... same note") arrives after both quotes. For a first-time listener this is too late; the impression of direct prophetic alignment is already registered. The brief required the short to "say this out loud" so it never implies word-for-word prophecy. The current order weakens that protection.

**4. "The last word Jesus spoke on the cross" is factually imprecise.**

The script repeatedly calls the John 19:30 utterance "the last word Jesus spoke on the cross" (hook and transition). Luke 23:46 records Jesus speaking after this ("Father, into thy hands I commend my spirit"). The claim is only true inside John's Gospel, not across the canonical record. Given the brief's "ACCURACY #1" emphasis and the care taken on the Hebrew/Greek word distinction, this is an unnecessary and contestable overstatement.

**5. Hook does not grip in the first ~5 seconds for the target audience/format.**

The opening is a flat literary observation about two ancient texts landing on the same idea. No story entry, no visceral image, no question, no immediate tension. "The last line of Psalm twenty-two..." requires the listener to already care about Psalm 22's structure. For a 60s gospel Short that must work for zero-Bible-knowledge viewers, this is a weak hook by the stated criterion.

**6. Minor but real looseness in the prose setup around the Psalm quote.**

"David ends the song looking ahead — to God's saving work, accomplished, and told to a people not yet born."

The KJV clause is "unto a people that shall be born, that he hath done this." "Not yet born" is a reasonable compression, but the surrounding narration is already carrying heavy interpretive weight ("looking ahead — to God's saving work"). Small inaccuracies in the non-quoted framing compound when the whole point of the short is precision on what is and is not being claimed.

No transaction/debt language survived (good). No "three words" count (good). The grace landing ("Nothing left for you to earn, nothing left to prove, nothing to add. He has done it — all of it.") is clean. The one-thread spine holds. But the punctuation error is exactly the class of residual the brief flagged, and the clarity/order problems are first-hearing defects on a piece that must stand alone in 60 seconds.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix John 19:30 spoken quote to place any terminal period strictly outside the quotation marks with no non-KJV punctuation presented inside ("It is finished".); correct the matching claim in DEPTH & SOURCING.
2. Replace or clarify "And when Jesus had carried the cross all the way to the end" so the "Done." → finished-work link is unmistakable on first hearing without evoking the physical via dolorosa.
3. Move the resonance guard earlier or weaken the pre-guard claims ("very same truth", "David ends the song looking ahead — to God's saving work, accomplished") so a zero-knowledge listener cannot register a direct prophetic fulfilment before the qualification arrives.
