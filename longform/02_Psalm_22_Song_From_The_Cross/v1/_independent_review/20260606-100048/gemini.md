# Independent review — gemini (OK, 54s)

Here is the independent critique based on the provided LENS criteria:

**1. FIRST-HEARING CLARITY FLAW: The "Seven Words" Error (Movement 1)**
*Cite: "screamed seven words into the dark." followed by "My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?"*
*Critique:* This is a glaring factual and audio-dissonance error. The script explicitly tells the listener to expect seven words, but the KJV quote that immediately follows contains exactly **nine** words. (The original Aramaic phrase *Eli, Eli, lema sabachthani* is four words). The writer has mistakenly conflated the theological tradition of the "Seven Last Words of Christ" (meaning His seven *sayings* from the cross) with the literal word count of this specific cry. A listener will instinctively count the words, realize the math is wrong, and drop out of the narrative. 
*Fix:* Remove the word count. Change to "screamed into the dark" or "screamed his desperate question into the dark."

**2. FIRST-HEARING CLARITY FLAW: The "One Word" Error (Movement 7)**
*Cite: "Jesus receives the vinegar, and says one word: [jesus] 'It is finished.'"*
*Critique:* The writer commits the exact same word-counting error here. Yes, in the original Greek, it is a single word (*tetelestai*), but the script uses the KJV English, which is **three** words. Telling a listener with zero Bible/Greek knowledge that someone "says one word" and then immediately playing an audio clip of a voice saying three words ("It is finished") creates completely unnecessary confusion right at the emotional climax of the film. 
*Fix:* Simply say "Jesus receives the vinegar, and says:" or "Jesus receives the vinegar, and declares a finished work:".

**3. DOCTRINE & FRESHNESS (Praise/Observation)**
*Cite: "One disputed word does not carry that. The convergence does." (Movement 4) and "not with what you must do, but with what he hath done." (Movement 7)*
*Critique:* Excluding the word-count errors, the architecture of the script is exceptionally strong. Movement 4 brilliantly steel-mans the "pierced" textual variant without surrendering the apologetic argument. Movement 7 achieves the "Freshness = faithful depth" mandate beautifully by linking Psalm 22:31 (*"he hath done this"*) with John 19:30 (*"It is finished"*). The landing successfully avoids cliché, perfectly adhering to the grace-anchored mandate.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Movement 1: Remove "seven words" — the KJV quote is nine words. Change to "screamed into the dark."
2. Movement 7: Remove "says one word" — the English KJV quote is three words. Change to "and says:" or "and declares:".
3. Verify no other cross-translation word counts were used as setup dialogue; relying on Greek/Aramaic word counts to introduce English quotes ruins audio clarity.
