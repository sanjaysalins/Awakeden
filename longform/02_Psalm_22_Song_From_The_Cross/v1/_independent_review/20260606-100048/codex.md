# Independent review — codex (OK, 100s)

Findings:

1. **Wrong hook word count.** “Jesus of Nazareth gathered his strength and screamed seven words” is false against the quoted KJV English: “My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me?” is nine words. If counting the Aramaic cry, it is not seven either.

2. **Doctrinally risky wording around Christ’s cry.** “We hear despair. And it is despair.” risks implying sinful hopelessness in Christ. Better category is agony, dereliction, lament, or forsakenness. “Despair” is theologically loaded.

3. **Overclaim on resurrection from Psalm 22:22.** “A dead man does not declare anything. The sufferer is alive again” is too blunt from the psalm alone. Psalm 22 can read as rescue from near death unless you bring Hebrews 2:12 into the spoken argument. The ledger knows this; the narration does not.

4. **KJV quote-policy issue in Movement 4.** If every word inside quotation marks must be KJV, then “like a lion at my hands and feet” and “they dug” violate the rule. They are textual-discussion glosses, not KJV. Use italics or explicit paraphrase formatting.

5. **Overstated incipit claim.** “When a first-century Jew quoted the opening line of a psalm, he was not quoting a line. He was naming the whole song” is too absolute. He was quoting a line and invoking the psalm. The current wording sounds like a logic trick.

6. **Unverifiable invented claim.** “The soldiers throwing dice had never read Psalm twenty-two” cannot be known. Likely, but not provable. This is exactly the sort of flourish that creates avoidable credibility risk.

7. **The ‘pierced’ fairness gets partially undone later.** Movement 4 says, “Take pierced off the table entirely,” but Movement 5 says, “He was claiming the pierced hands as his own hands” without reintroducing the textual caution. That feels rhetorically convenient.

8. **Forced “finished work” linkage needs restraint.** “the song ends on three words” conflicts with the quoted “he hath done this,” which is four words, and Psalm 22:31 in KJV ends with “that he hath done this.” The John 19:30 resonance is legitimate, but this line oversells the neatness.

9. **Ledger contains a sourcing overclaim.** “John 19:24, 19:28 explicitly cite Psalm 22” is not clean. John 19:24 explicitly cites Psalm 22:18; John 19:28’s “I thirst” is commonly tied to Psalm 69:21, not explicitly Psalm 22.

10. **Possible first-hearing confusion.** “Jesus receives the vinegar, and says one word: ‘It is finished.’” The KJV English quote is three words. If you mean Greek *tetelestai*, say that outside the KJV quote structure.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Fix the doctrinally risky “despair” language and tighten the forsaking/penal-substitution wording.
2. Bring Hebrews 2:12 into the spoken resurrection argument or soften the Psalm 22:22 resurrection claim.
3. Clean the quote-policy and precision issues: “seven words,” non-KJV quotation marks, “one word,” and “three words.”
