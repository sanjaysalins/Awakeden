## The 7-movement spine (per long, adapted from the Isaiah 53 pilot)

1. **The Picture** — set the OT scene plainly, as a first-time listener would meet it.
2. **The Problem it answers** — why this ritual/sign existed; the human need underneath.
3. **The Strange Detail** — the freshness hook: the odd, over-specific feature that begs explanation.
4. **The Centuries-Early Match** — walk the OT text verbatim against the Gospel record.
5. **The Honest Objection** — steel-man the "coincidence / over-reading" pushback, then answer it.
6. **The Exchange** — the substitution heart: what it cost, who it was for (grace-anchored).
7. **The Invitation** — landing on Christ; the Spirit convicts, the script invites (no fear/gain-loss).

## Per-topic spine + spinoff shorts

### 1 — The Passover Lamb (Exodus 12)
- **Strange detail:** blood on the *doorposts* (not the altar); the lamb *kept 4 days* then killed; **not a bone broken**; eaten in haste, ready to leave.
- **Match:** John 19:33–36 (legs unbroken, "that the scripture should be fulfilled"); 1 Cor 5:7; lamb "without blemish" → 1 Pet 1:19; the firstborn spared by blood.
- **Objection:** "every culture has sacrifice" → answer: the *specificity* (doorpost, unbroken bone, kept-then-slain, the LORD's own Passover *over* the blood).
- **Shorts (4):** the unbroken bone · blood on the doorposts ("when I see the blood") · the lamb kept 4 days = examined & found faultless · "Christ our passover" (1 Cor 5:7).

### 2 — The Bronze Serpent (Numbers 21)
- **Strange detail:** healing comes by *looking* at a bronze image of the very thing that's killing them — lifted on a pole.
- **Match:** John 3:14–15 — **Jesus applies it to himself**; "lifted up" = the cross (John 12:32–33); look-and-live = believe-and-live.
- **Objection:** "isn't that an idol?" → answer: it healed by God's word + the look of faith, not the metal (cf. 2 Kgs 18:4 when it became an idol it was destroyed).
- **Shorts (3):** "look and live" · the thing that killed them, lifted up · "even so must the Son of man be lifted up."

### 3 — The Seed of the Woman (Genesis 3:15)
- **Strange detail:** in the curse, the **first promise of a rescuer** — the *woman's* seed (not the man's), a bruised heel that still crushes the head.
- **Match:** Gal 4:4 "made of a woman"; Rom 16:20 "bruise Satan under your feet"; the virgin birth (Isa 7:14 echo); Rev 12.
- **Objection:** "reading Jesus into a snake story" → answer: the trajectory of the whole canon turns here; enmity, seed, head-wound vs heel-wound.
- **Shorts (4):** the first gospel in the curse · "her seed" (why the woman's) · heel vs head — the cost & the victory · the serpent-crusher promised in Eden.

### 4 — Day of Atonement / Scapegoat (Leviticus 16)
- **Strange detail:** **two** goats for one sin offering — one slain, one driven *alive* into the wilderness carrying the sin away; the high priest alone, once a year, behind the veil.
- **Match:** Heb 9–10 (better blood, once for all, the veil); Isa 53:6; the torn veil (Mt 27:51).
- **Objection:** "why two goats / which one is Jesus?" → answer: one act, two truths — penalty paid *and* guilt carried away ("as far as the east is from the west").
- **Shorts (3):** the goat that carried it away · the blood behind the veil · "once for all" (the veil torn).

### 5 — Melchizedek (Genesis 14 + Psalm 110)
- **Strange detail:** a **king who is also a priest** (forbidden in the Law), no recorded genealogy, brings out **bread and wine**, blesses Abraham — then vanishes from the record.
- **Match:** Ps 110:4 + Heb 5–7 — a priesthood *greater than Aaron's*, "for ever," that Jesus fills; bread & wine foreshadow.
- **Objection:** "obscure / a stretch" → answer: it's the OT's own answer to "how can Messiah be both King (of Judah) and Priest?" — Psalm 110 already says so.
- **Shorts (3):** the king-priest the Law couldn't make · bread and wine before the cross · "a priest for ever."
