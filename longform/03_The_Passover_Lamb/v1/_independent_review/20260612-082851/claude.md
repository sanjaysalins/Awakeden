## Real problems

**1. The "whole assembly" detail is set up, then abandoned (thread coherence).**
Movement 3 gives it heavy weight: *"the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill it"* → *"Not one executioner — the whole people share the act."* It's listed as one of the "strangely over-specified" clues. But Movement 4 (the Match) and Movement 5 (the Objection recap: *"spotless… inspected… doorframe of wood… bone-rule… timing"*) **never cash it out.** In a typology piece whose entire argument is precise correspondence, raising a clue and giving it no fulfillment payoff quietly undercuts your own case. Either connect it (all of us / the whole guilty assembly at the cross) or drop the emphasis.

**2. "Four hundred years of bondage" — imprecise (Movement 1).**
The sojourn was 430 years (Ex 12:40); the 400 is the *affliction* foretold (Gen 15:13 / Acts 7:6), and the slavery itself didn't span the whole period (it began after Joseph's generation). "Four hundred years of slavery" overstates. Safer: "centuries of bondage" or "four hundred years of affliction."

**3. The close leans on judgment-avoidance — watch the grace anchor (Movement 7).**
*"let the judgment pass over you"* / *"what will He see? Your striving — or the blood of the Lamb?"* The works-vs-blood contrast is genuinely grace-anchored, good. But the surrounding pull is "escape the death coming to your door," which is the Passover's real content yet edges toward self-interest framing. It currently stays on the right side because the invite is *receive the substitute*, not *do this or else*. Flagging it as a line to hold, not a fail — don't let any revision tip it into "save yourself."

## Minor

- **Pilate as "inspector" (Movement 4)** is a slight stretch — *"no fault"* is legal innocence, not the lamb's moral spotlessness. Defensible typology, but the deeper inspection is His sinless life; leaning on Pilate alone understates it.
- **Hook (Movement 1 opening)** — *"It is the last night of Israel's slavery in Egypt"* is expository, not a grab. The real hook (*"they will sound oddly precise… something — or someone — else"*) lands at the end of M1. Fine for long-form's slower burn, but the first sentence does no gripping work.

Doctrine is sound throughout (substitution stated cleanly: *"It died instead of the firstborn… God providing the substitute Himself"*), the Israelites-aren't-auto-safe point in M2 is a strong, faithful read, and the landing does new work. The fixes are tightening, not rebuilding.

VERDICT: REVISE

2. Correct "four hundred years of slavery" to the affliction/sojourn distinction (430 sojourn; 400 affliction; slavery not the full span).
3. Hold the Movement 7 close grace-anchored — keep it "receive the substitute," never let the judgment-avoidance pull become self-interest pressure.
