# Independent review — gemini (OK, 43s)

Here is the independent, adversarial review of the draft script:

**KJV Verbatim Violations**
*   **Movement 2:** `"I will pass through the land of Egypt this night," God says, "and will smite all the firstborn."` 
    *   **Critique:** Fails strict verbatim check. Exodus 12:12 begins with *"For I will pass..."* The script omits the word "For". Additionally, the script silently truncates the end of the verse without an ellipsis (cutting *"in the land of Egypt, both man and beast"*). 
*   **Movement 3:** `"They shall take of the blood, and strike it..."` 
    *   **Critique:** Fails strict verbatim check. Exodus 12:7 begins with *"And they shall take of the blood..."* The script omits the word "And".

**Clarity on First Hearing**
*   **Movement 3:** `"the whole assembly of the congregation of Israel shall kill it in the evening." Not one executioner — the whole people share the act.`
    *   **Critique:** A listener with zero Bible knowledge was told in Movement 1: "Every household, one lamb." Suddenly, they are told "the whole assembly... shall kill it." The unchurched listener will picture two million Israelites crowded around a single lamb trying to stab it at the same time. The script fails to clarify that the assembly is acting collectively to slay *their respective* lambs at the exact same twilight hour.

**Grace-Anchored Conviction & Landing**
*   **Movement 7:** `A family could believe the lamb was spotless, agree every rule was kept — and still lose the firstborn, if the blood was never put on their own door.`
    *   **Critique:** This directly violates the "NO fear / gain-loss / self-interest" rule. You are leveraging the visceral fear of losing a child to pressure the listener into a decision. It shifts the tone from the Spirit's conviction of Christ's sufficiency to a threat of loss. 
*   **Movement 7 (Landing):** `When God looks at your life, what will He see? Your striving — or the blood of the Lamb?... Come under the blood of the Lamb who was slain for you — and let the judgment pass over.`
    *   **Critique:** This violates the "Landing does NEW work" rule. This is exactly the "tired generic 'will you trust Him?' close" explicitly forbidden by the prompt, merely reskinned with Passover vocabulary. It does no new conceptual work. A truly fresh, grace-anchored landing would focus on the unmerited peace *inside* the house—pointing out that inside the house, the Israelites didn't have to be brave, worthy, or without doubt; their safety relied entirely on the blood outside the door, not their feelings inside it.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Correct the KJV quote discrepancies in Movement 2 (Ex 12:12, missing "For" and missing ellipsis) and Movement 3 (Ex 12:7, missing "And").
2. Rewrite the Movement 7 landing to eliminate the fear/gain-loss framing ("lose the firstborn") and replace the generic altar call with an ending that does NEW theological work about resting in grace.
3. Clarify the "whole assembly shall kill it" phrasing in Movement 3 so the unchurched listener doesn't visualize a nation executing a single animal.
