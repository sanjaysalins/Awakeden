## Engine self-assessment — where each draft is weakest

**(a) The hunger bread can't fill — John 6:35**
- [Theologian] John 6:35's "never hunger / never thirst" is *spiritual* satisfaction in Christ. The landing "you never go hungry again" must clearly mean the soul, not the removal of all earthly/emotional longing — guard against an over-promise (scene-scope, rule 6).
- [Skeptic/Clarity] "the gap is shaped like Him" is a near-conceit — does it land on ONE hearing for a zero-Bible viewer, or read as writerly? (rule 2)
- [G3 landing] Risks ending on benefit-to-me ("never go hungry again") rather than WHO CHRIST IS — confirm it lands on the Bread Himself, not just my relief (rule 3).

**(b) Manna fulfilled — John 6:51**
- [Theologian / dropped-grace-gift] The quote truncates 6:51 BEFORE its own gospel payoff — "and the bread that I will give is my flesh, which I will give for the life of the world." That dropped clause IS the cross. Same trap as #31 (truncating before the grace inside the verse). Decide: keep the truncation or carry the flesh-given clause.
- [Scene-scope] "the grave defeated" — is resurrection in-scope here? (John 6:40,44 "raise him up at the last day" supports it in-chapter; confirm it's not an over-reach on 6:51 alone.) (rule 6)
- [Clarity] "the hunger of death itself" — check it parses on one hearing.

**(c) In no wise cast out — John 6:37**
- [Theologian] 6:37 has TWO halves: "All that the Father giveth me shall come to me" (the Father's giving / election) AND the assurance "him that cometh... in no wise cast out." The narration leans only on the second. Does quoting the first half raise an unanswered election question for a skeptic? Consider quoting only the assurance clause vs. the whole verse. (rule 1 verbatim is fine either way.)
- [Pacing rule 8] Beyond the full-verse quote, the draft re-quotes "him that cometh" and "in no wise cast out" as short echoes — confirm these don't read as extra spoken quotes that rush the narrator.
- [Landing rule 7] "He will not turn you away" — tied to the verse so fairly un-portable; confirm it's not a tired closer.

## The three current drafts

**(a) THE HUNGER BREAD CAN'T FILL**
- Hook — You can be completely full and still feel empty. Fridge stocked, day went fine — and something inside is still hungry.
- Point — That ache isn't a flaw to fix. It's a signpost. You were built to be filled by Someone, not something.
- Proof — Hear Him: "I am the bread of life: he that cometh to me shall never hunger; and he that believeth on me shall never thirst." Not less hungry. Never.
- Conviction — So you keep reaching for the next thing — the next plate, the next purchase, the next person — hoping this one finally closes the gap. It never does, because the gap is shaped like Him.
- Landing — Stop feeding a soul-hunger with things that spoil. Come to the Bread of life. He doesn't take the edge off — He fills you, and you never go hungry again.

**(b) MANNA FULFILLED**
- Hook — In the desert, God rained bread from heaven every morning. The people gathered it, ate, lived another day — and still, every one of them died.
- Point — The manna kept them alive; it couldn't keep them from the grave. It was a picture, waiting for the real Bread.
- Proof — Hear Him: "I am the living bread which came down from heaven: if any man eat of this bread, he shall live for ever..." Manna fed them till sundown. This Bread feeds you forever.
- Conviction — You treat Jesus like manna — enough for today's crisis, then back to the cupboard tomorrow. But He isn't a daily top-up. He's the Bread that ends the hunger of death itself.
- Landing — The fathers ate and died. Eat this Bread — come to Him — and you shall live for ever. That's not a better breakfast. That's the grave defeated.

**(c) IN NO WISE CAST OUT**
- Hook — Somewhere in you is the quiet fear that if you actually came to God, He'd look at your record and turn you away.
- Point — That fear keeps you at the door, rehearsing whether you're allowed in. Jesus answers it before you can ask.
- Proof — Hear Him: "All that the Father giveth me shall come to me; and him that cometh to me I will in no wise cast out." In no wise. Not maybe. Not if you clean up first.
- Conviction — You think your case might be the exception — too far gone, too late, too much. But "him that cometh" has no fine print. The only way to be cast out is to never come.
- Landing — So come. Bring the record you're ashamed of. The One who said "in no wise cast out" has never once broken His word. Come to Him — He will not turn you away.
