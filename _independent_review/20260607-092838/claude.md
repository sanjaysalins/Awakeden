# Independent review — claude (OK, 199s)

I've verified the plan against the actual codebase. The claims are mostly accurate, but verification surfaced several concrete problems the plan glosses over. Here are my findings.

## Findings

**1. The plan's central empirical anchor is half-false — and its acceptance test will fail.**
Premise #3 and the re-verification step both assert the cache "dropped the Ps 22:7 comma and elided Acts 8:35," and the test in *Verification* says these "will surface." I checked `data/kjv_cache.json`:
- **Acts 8:35 is currently clean/verbatim** in the cache: `"Then Philip opened his mouth, and began at the same scripture, and preached unto him Jesus."` There is no elision. So the Fix 3/re-verify acceptance criterion "Acts 8:35 elision will surface" **cannot pass** — it's already been repaired (or never persisted to cache).
- **Ps 22:7 is still corrupt, but not where the plan says.** Cache has `"...they shake the head saying,"` — the real KJV is `"...they shake the head, saying,"` (comma after *head*). The dropped comma is after "head," not "after scorn." The plan mislocates its own flagship bug.

This matters: the plan cites two reproducible corruptions to justify the heaviest fix (Fix 1), and one of the two doesn't reproduce. Whoever panels this should demand a *fresh* `_audit_kjv_cache` dry-run listing the divergences that **actually still exist** before greenlighting a full offline-canon build.

**2. Fix 1's "authoritative canon" has unverified provenance — it may swap one wrong source for another.** The design says "pulls a vetted public-domain 1769 KJV" and verifies "66 books / 31,102 verses / checksum." But digital KJV texts diverge precisely on **punctuation** (1611 vs 1769 Blayney vs modern Cambridge/Oxford settings differ in commas/colons — the exact thing Fix 1 claims to fix). The checksum proves the file is *unchanged*, not *correct*. The plan never names the canonical edition or how its punctuation is itself validated. This is a single point of failure: the entire Scripture-integrity guarantee rests on a source whose correctness is asserted, not verified. Fix this by naming the exact edition and spot-checking N known-hard verses (Ps 22:7, Matt 8:27 "!" etc.) against a human-trusted printed reference before trusting the canon.

**3. Fix 2 ("the keystone") underweights a hard, lossy adapter.** The plan calls it a "small adapter in pipeline/ to lift a narration.md into the review models" and says "prefer the engine panel so G1–G8.6 apply uniformly." Verified: `engine.review()` requires a fully structured `Draft` — it unpacks `draft.beats`, `hook_type`, `beat_ids`, `scripture_quoted`, `word_count`, etc. Reconstructing **beats and beat boundaries from finished flat prose is lossy and inferential**, and gates like G5 (Structure Conformance) and the timed five-beat checks are *defined on* those boundaries. An adapter that guesses beat splits will make G5 verdicts on fabricated structure — worse than not running it. This is not "small." Either (a) scope Fix 2's LLM layer to `independent_review.LENS_NARRATION` (which works on raw prose) and drop the "reuse engine panel" ambition, or (b) budget the adapter honestly as its own risky sub-project.

**4. The reused spoken-extractor is long-form-shaped and won't work on shorts as-is.** Verified `veed_io/_extract_spoken.py`: it's **CLI-only** (hard-coded `sys.argv[1/2]`, no importable function) and keeps text **only inside `## MOVEMENT` sections** — that's the long-form format. Fix 2 explicitly targets "long + short." Short narration.md files don't use `## MOVEMENT` headers, so the extractor will likely return **empty string** on a short, silently passing every downstream check. The plan's "generalize if needed" buries a real, format-specific gap. Confirm the shorts' actual structure and write/justify a shared extractor before assuming reuse.

**5. Sequencing is backwards for proving the idea.** The plan's own *Verification* section calls the Fix 3 deterministic cluster layer "the acceptance test for the whole effort" — a ~$0 offline n-gram/Jaccard pass that would have caught all four repetition patterns (8× "Come to Him", 6× "thousand years," etc.). That is the cheapest fix and the one that directly addresses the actual miss. Yet it's sequenced **third**, behind a full offline-Bible build (Fix 1) and a lossy adapter (Fix 2). Build and validate the Fix 3 deterministic layer **first** — it proves the thesis in an afternoon, is zero-cost, zero-risk, and de-risks whether the rest is even worth building. This is the over-engineering tell: 5 fixes / ~10 new files / multiple `engine.py` edits committed before the core hypothesis is demonstrated.

**6. Fix 4 has a convergence/portability hazard the plan doesn't address.** A *dynamic* phrase ledger fed as "diverge from these" across a 76-short catalogue will, at scale, start banning legitimately shared gospel vocabulary — "the cross," "trust Him," "Christ" recur because the gospel recurs. Without a similarity *threshold* and a whitelist of doctrinally-load-bearing phrases, Fix 4 will either (a) push generation toward novelty-for-novelty's-sake (the exact freshness-over-faithfulness trap CLAUDE.md warns against) or (b) get ignored. The plan specifies the *mechanism* (jsonl, append, `used()`) but not the *policy* (what counts as repetition vs. shared-gospel-core). That policy is the hard part.

**7. Minor: "validates against the corruption and passes" is overstated.** Verified `verbatim_mismatches` compares quotes against the `passage` argument and **deliberately skips** spans with <70% overlap as "paraphrase or cross-ref" — that NT-skip is an intentional false-positive guard, not pure negligence. The plan frames it as a pure oversight; the fix (verify cross-refs against the full canon) is right, but the diagnosis should acknowledge the skip is load-bearing and removing it risks false FAILs on legitimate paraphrase frames (attribution lines in narrator voice).

## What's genuinely good
The root-cause analysis is correct and structural (per-artifact review can't see set-level repetition — true; hand-authored content bypasses all engine gates — verified true). Fix 3's deterministic layer is the right, cheap, high-value core. The "$0/no-metered-API" cost posture is honest and the re-render estimate (~$0–$3) is realistic. Review discipline (red-team + panel on the plan and the build) follows the standing rule.
