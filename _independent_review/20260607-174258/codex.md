# Independent review — codex (OK, 104s)

KJV spot-check: the quoted words appear verbatim KJV; I did not find an altered quoted word. Checked representative KJV text for Psalm 22 and Hebrews/John: [Psalm 22:18](https://biblehub.com/kjv/psalms/22-18.htm), [Psalm 22:27](https://biblehub.com/kjv/psalms/22-27.htm), [Hebrews 2:11](https://www.christianity.com/bible/kjv/hebrews/2-11), [John 19:30](https://www.bibleref.com/John/19/John-19-30.html).

01 PASS. “His life, laid down to buy you back” is grace-anchored, Christ-centered, and distinct.

02 PASS. Prior issue resolved. “He could have come down in an instant — He chose to stay” rejects powerlessness and lands on voluntary substitution.

03 PASS. “This was no cry of lost faith” is needed and sound. CTA is invitational, not works-framed.

04 FLAG. The repair mostly works, but “And hear what He calls us… — brothers. Family.” still risks applying “brethren” to the whole listening audience before conversion. “His own brethren” helps, and “There’s room in that word for you” is invitational, but the earlier “us” blurs the redeemed/general-audience distinction. Also, “Hebrews takes that opening line — word for word” is too broad if hearers take it to mean the whole quoted Psalm line; Hebrews’ KJV wording differs in the second clause.

05 PASS. Prior issue resolved. “Nothing — only Someone to come home to: the One who said it is done” closes on Christ’s finished work, not the viewer’s effort.

06 PASS. Prior endpoint issue resolved. “swept the whole earth” is a little broad, but the CTA keeps the listener inside the larger nations thread rather than making them the prophecy’s endpoint.

07 FLAG. “He was broken in your place” is orthodox as sacrificial shorthand, but in this specific short it follows “all my bones are out of joint” and “I may tell all my bones,” so first-hearing clarity may imply broken bones. That collides with the Johannine emphasis that His bones were not broken.

08 FLAG. “And that water is a Person” is clever but less clear than it needs to be. It may confuse a zero-Bible listener, and doctrinally it blurs whether the “living water” is Christ, His gift, or the Spirit. The body points to Christ, but the CTA lands on an unnamed metaphor.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Tighten #04 so “brethren/family” applies only to Christ’s own, with invitation clearly phrased as becoming included, not already being included.
2. Replace or clarify #08’s “that water is a Person” so the close names Christ plainly and avoids living-water ambiguity.
3. Reword #07’s “He was broken” to avoid implying Jesus’ bones were broken.
