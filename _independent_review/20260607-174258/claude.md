## TWO new problems

**1. #04 and #06 share an ending — violates requirement (5).**
- #04: *"There's room in that word **for you**."*
- #06: *"still has room **for you** to turn to Him."*

Both land on the same distinctive image — *"room … for you."* This is the same class of collision the prior round flagged (#01/#02 both ending "for you"); it's recurred between #04 and #06. Differentiate one — #04's "room in that word" is the more original, so I'd reword #06's close.

**2. #08 "died of thirst" is imprecise.**
*"The God who made every ocean **died of thirst** — so He could hand you the one water that never runs dry."* Jesus thirsted on the cross, but He did not die *from* thirst — He died by crucifixion and voluntarily gave up His spirit (John 19:30). A hostile listener (or critic) catches this. Suggest *"thirsted unto death"* or *"the God who made every ocean cried out in thirst."* Also *"And that water is a Person"* is slightly cryptic for a zero-Bible listener, but it points to Christ — acceptable as-is.

Everything else (forms vary, grace-anchored, each points to Christ, hooks grip) holds. No cheese, no fear/gain-loss framing.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Reword #06's close so it no longer shares "room … for you" with #04 (requirement 5 not yet met).
2. Fix #08 "died of thirst" → He thirsted but died by crucifixion, not of thirst (accuracy).
3. Optional: sharpen #08's "that water is a Person" so a first-time hearer clearly catches that the water is Christ.
