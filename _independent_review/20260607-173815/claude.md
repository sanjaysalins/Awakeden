# Independent review — claude (OK, 87s)

I reviewed only the CTAs and the flow into them. KJV quotes and the doctrinal middle I took as verified per the brief. Findings below, by short.

## Per-short

**#01 — "…Himself, for you."**
- Form (reversal/irony) and grace-anchor are sound; lands on Christ-as-gift. Fine.
- Minor: "They rolled dice" glosses "cast lots." Lots ≠ dice strictly. Colloquial, acceptable, but a literalist will flag it. Low priority.

**#02 — "…He wouldn't. He stayed, for you."**
- Special check (b): The short QUOTES the 22:8 / Matt 27:43 thread — *"let him deliver him."* The CTA then pivots to *"couldn't save Himself"* (Matt 27:42), a mockery line **never established in this short**. It's the same scene and the prior line ("not because Jesus lacked the power to come down") grounds it thematically, so it's a *fair* reversal — but there's a small logical seam: the listener is handed a taunt ("couldn't save Himself") the script didn't actually show. Tighten by tying the reversal to the line you DID quote ("They said 'let him deliver him' — and He wouldn't"), or quote/echo 27:42 earlier.
- **Set problem:** #01 and #02 BOTH close on the identical cadence *", for you."* Back-to-back. That is the start of a new shared template — exactly what this rewrite was meant to avoid. Change one.

**#03 — "…He opened it from the dark."**
- Grace-anchored, lands on Christ ("He opened it"). "However far you've run" is invitation, not pressure. Good. No issue.

**#04 — "…called it brethren — and there's room in that word for you."**
- **Top doctrinal flag.** Hebrews 2:11–13 has Christ "not ashamed to call **them** brethren" — "them" = those He sanctifies / the children God gave Him, i.e. His own. The CTA says the risen Christ "looked at **the world that nailed Him down** and called **it** brethren." As written that extends "brethren" to the hostile world indiscriminately — a universalism-adjacent overclaim. The closing clause "there's room in that word for you" is the correct invitation, but the preceding claim overstates the text. Fix: make brethren those who come to Him (e.g. "…and opened that word wide enough for you"), so the invitation carries the reach, not a blanket declaration.

**#05 — "…what are you still trying to finish?"**
- Special check (a): Reads as **grace, not works-pressure.** The setup ("nothing left to earn, to prove, to add") frames the question as an invitation to *stop* striving — it indicts self-effort, doesn't demand it. Fresh, not a tired bare question. Passes.
- Caveat: the final beat lands on the viewer's striving rather than on Christ-the-Person; Christ is present only as the antecedent "He." Acceptable because the finished WORK is His, but it's the second-weakest on "lands unmistakably on Christ."

**#06 — "…you are the ends of the earth it was singing toward."**
- Special check (c): Sound application, not overreach. Ps 22:27's "all the ends of the world" legitimately includes the listener; the gospel's actual historical spread is the warrant, which the middle establishes. Fine.
- Caveat: like #05, the last sentence lands on "you," not on Christ the Person (Christ is "one man dying alone"). Viewer-reframe form is allowed, but as a SET, #04/#05/#06 all close on "…you." Watch the drift toward a "you"-ending tic.

**#07 — "He came apart to hold you together."**
- Strong paradox, Christ-centered, grace. Borderline cute but earns it off the "bones out of joint" quote. Keep.

**#08 — "The well that never runs dry is a Person."**
- Best of the set on "lands on Christ as a Person" — explicit. Sound (John 4 echo). Only nit: three stacked thirst-reversals ("went dry" / "died of thirst" / "well that never runs dry") is slightly redundant; could trim the middle sentence. Quality, not doctrine.

## Set-level
- Register variety IS genuinely there (declarative / reversal / grace / question / reframe / paradox). Good.
- BUT two emerging shared tics: (1) the em-dash-then-turn structure recurs in #01/#03/#04/#08; (2) the closing words — *"for you"* (#01, #02 consecutive), *"you"* (#04, #06), *"you together"* (#07) — five of eight resolve on "you." The single hard repeat to fix is **#01 and #02 ending on the exact same ", for you."**

Net: grace-anchored throughout, no fear/gain-loss/works framing, all point to Christ. Two real fixes (#04 overclaim, #01/#02 identical close) plus the #02 off-quote seam keep this from a clean pass.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. #04 — reword "looked at the world that nailed Him down and called it brethren" so "brethren" isn't extended to the hostile world wholesale (overclaim vs. Heb 2:11–13's "them" = those He sanctifies); let "room/room for you" carry the invitation instead.
2. #01 + #02 — both close on the identical ", for you." Rewrite one ending; that repeat is a new shared template the rewrite was meant to kill.
3. #02 — anchor the "couldn't/wouldn't" reversal to the taunt you actually quoted ("let him deliver him"), since "couldn't save Himself" (Matt 27:42) is never shown in the short.
