# Independent review — codex (OK, 61s)

**Findings**

#01: Pass. “Himself, for you” lands clearly on Christ’s self-giving, not viewer effort. The flow from “the cross was no accident. It was the plan” into the CTA is coherent.

#02: Mostly pass. “They jeered that He couldn't save Himself… He wouldn't” is a fair Matt. 27:42 reversal, not quoted as KJV, and it stays on the mockery/deliverance thread. Minor clarity issue: “And He wasn't delivered” could sound absolute; He was not delivered down from the cross, but He was vindicated/resurrected.

#03: Revise. “He was forsaken so that you never will be” is too absolute for an undifferentiated viewer. Evangelically, that promise belongs to those in Christ. “However far you've run, the way home is open” is grace-invitation, but the line before it risks universalizing assurance.

#04: Revise. “The risen Christ looked at the world that nailed Him down and called it brethren” overclaims. Hebrews’ “not ashamed to call them brethren” refers to those sanctified/brought into His family, not “the world” indiscriminately. “There’s room in that word for you” works as invitation, but the first half needs doctrinal narrowing.

#05: Pass. “What are you still trying to finish?” does not read as works-pressure because it follows “nothing left to earn, to prove, to add.” It challenges striving from finished-work grace. This is a fresh question, not the tired bare “will you trust Him?” form.

#06: Borderline. “You are the ends of the earth it was singing toward” is strong but over-personalized. The psalm points to the nations/ends of the world, and “includes wherever you are” is sound; “you are the ends” makes the viewer the endpoint more than Christ’s worldwide reign. Fresh, but a little sentimental and theologically inflated.

#07: Revise. “He came apart to hold you together” is the weakest close. “Came apart” may imply physical disintegration, while the text says “all my bones are out of joint,” not that His body came apart. “Hold you together” also lands as therapeutic/vague rather than clearly gospel salvation.

#08: Revise lightly. “The well that never runs dry is a Person” is a good Christ-centered close. But the lead-in “The God who fills every ocean died of thirst” is an overstatement; John records “I thirst,” but crucifixion death is not reducible to dying of thirst. “The thirst in you” also risks vague felt-need language, though the Person landing helps.

No KJV word alterations stood out in the quoted material provided; the issues are mainly doctrinal precision and CTA landing, not quotation accuracy.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Correct #04’s overextension of “brethren” to “the world that nailed Him down”; Hebrews applies that word to those brought into Christ’s family, not humanity indiscriminately.
2. Replace vague or physically imprecise closes, especially #07 “He came apart to hold you together” and #08 “died of thirst.”
3. Soften over-personalized direct address in #03/#06 so the invitation remains grace-anchored without implying every viewer already has the promise or is the psalm’s endpoint.
