# Independent adversarial review — Psalm 22 de-templated hooks + CTAs (set of 8)

Default stance: the middles were pre-verified; I still checked quoted spans against project KJV cache. Findings below focus on the **new hooks/CTAs**, set variety, thread/flow, and first-hear clarity.

---

## SET-LEVEL FINDING (most important)

De-templating removed the old shared open/close, but **a new shared CTA skeleton appeared**:

| Short | CTA |
|-------|-----|
| 02 | **Let it in.** |
| 04 | **Step into it.** |
| 06 | **Answer it.** |

Same shape: **imperative + vague pronoun, no Jesus, no beat-specific gospel work.** These are exactly the boilerplate risks named in the brief. Five shorts land with substance (01, 03, 05, 07, 08); **three do not.** That is not full de-templating success.

Hooks are genuinely varied. CTAs are **not** equally varied.

---

## 01 — The Crucifixion Foretold

**Hook:** *"Ten centuries before the first nail, a song already knew how the dying man's clothes would be divided."*  
**Verdict:** Strong ~5s grip; tied to Ps 22:18; no overclaim on the garments detail.

**CTA:** *"He was stripped so you could be clothed in Him — turn to Him."*  
Grace-anchored (clothing-in-Christ, not earn/prove). Lands on Jesus. *"turn to Him"* is slightly generic, but **"clothed in Him"** does new work and echoes the strip/divide thread. Acceptable.

**Thread/flow:** Hook → garments → John → plan → stripped/clothed. Spine holds.

**Minor:** Hook says *"Ten centuries"*; middle says *"A thousand years later"* / *"A thousand years early"* — not fatal, but the set already leans on millennium language in unchanged middles.

---

## 02 — The Mockers' Words

**Hook:** *"The taunts they would scream at the cross were written down, word for word, a millennium early."*  
**Problem — overclaim:** The middle itself walks this back: *"The same gesture. The same taunt"* and notes Ps 22:8 *"He trusted **on** the LORD"* vs Matt 27:43 *"He trusted **in** God"*. The hook’s **"word for word"** oversells what the script honestly argues. A hostile listener will hear contradiction, not freshness.

**CTA:** *"Let it in."*  
**Fail on landing criteria.** Antecedent is *"love they couldn't measure, and it is still reaching for you"* — but on one pass **"it"** is mush. No **Jesus** named. Could close a relationship podcast, a grief short, or a worship ad. **Too thin, not grace-anchored invitation to Christ** — just emotional assent. Weakest CTA in the set.

**Thread/flow:** Strong *"deliver"* spine through the conviction (*"staying was the only way to deliver you"*) then **drops the thread** for an pronoun with no object. De-templating broke the close here.

---

## 03 — The Forsaken Cry

**Hook:** *"The loneliest sentence Jesus ever spoke wasn't His own line — He was quoting."*  
Excellent grip; faithful (Jesus quotes Ps 22:1); surprising about the **text**, orthodox in claim.

**CTA:** *"He went into that dark to bring you home — so run home to the Father who waited."*  
Grace-anchored substitution → invitation. Prodigal echo fits Heb/brethren arc of the series. **"run home"** is the mildest urgency in the set (not fear/gain-loss), but it is still an action verb where 05’s *"rest"* is cleaner grace. Lands on the **Father** (acceptable Christward invitation). Does new work.

**Thread/flow:** forsaken cry → substitution → home. Holds.

**Watch:** *"He was forsaken so that you never will be"* is strong penal-substitution shorthand; orthodox for evangelical audience, but absolute for listeners who know suffering saints — edge case, not a fail.

---

## 04 — Declared To The Brethren

**Hook:** *"Everyone stops reading Psalm twenty-two at the cross."*  
Grips; sets up the turn honestly; NT-warranted in the middle (Hebrews).

**CTA:** *"Step into it."*  
**Weak.** Prior line: *"It ends not in a grave, but a family."* — so *"it"* is probably **family**, but the CTA never names **Jesus**, **brethren**, or **the risen Christ**. Same **[verb] + it** template as 02/06. Boilerplate. Does not do new gospel work on the landing; the family idea was already stated — the CTA adds almost nothing.

**Thread/flow:** cross → praise → risen Christ → brethren → family → vague step. **Flow dies on the last two words.**

---

## 05 — He Hath Done This

**Hook:** *"A psalm's last line and Jesus' last breath in John both close on the same note: finished."*  
Strong, accurate given the script’s explicit *"Different words… but the same note"* guard. Good model for 🟡 resonance beats.

**CTA:** *"There's nothing left for you to finish; rest in the One who said it is done."*  
**Best in set.** Anti-works (*"nothing left for you to earn, to prove, or to add"*), grace invitation (*"rest"*), beat-specific, echoes *finished/done*. **"the One"** is Christ-clear in context. Not cheesy.

**Thread/flow:** finished work → rest. Perfect spine.

---

## 06 — The Ends Of The Earth

**Hook:** *"It opens with one forsaken man dying alone. It ends with every nation on earth."*  
Clear contrast; true to Ps 22’s arc. Slightly less punchy than 01/03/08 (no named surprise in the first phrase), but workable in ~5s.

**CTA:** *"Answer it."*  
**Weak.** *"That song was reaching all the way to you. Answer it."* — Answer **what**? The prophecy? The call to *"turn unto the LORD"*? Personal inclusion in global worship? On first hearing this is a **logic gap**, not an invitation to **Jesus**. No name, no drink/rest/turn/home — just **[verb] it** again.

**Thread/flow:** nations → you personally → **non-sequitur close**. The middle’s *"turn unto the LORD"* deserved a CTA that echoes **turn**, not **answer**.

---

## 07 — The Body Foretold

**Hook:** *"A king described a body coming apart so precisely it reads like an autopsy of a crucifixion."*  
Grips hard. **"autopsy"** pushes toward sensational/medical click — the beat is descriptive match (🟡), not forensic proof. Hook slightly **overstates** epistemic humility the middle later applies (*"reads like"*, *"bears the marks of one"*).

**CTA:** *"Bring Him your broken places."*  
Solid grace invitation; lands on **Him**; ties body brokenness to Christ’s suffering without works framing. Does new work.

**Thread/flow:** body foretold → what Jesus bore → bring brokenness. Holds.

**Minor doctrine/discipline:** *"endured willingly, for you"* asserts volition; short 01 deliberately **removed** volition overclaim on the garments beat. Series inconsistency, not heresy.

---

## 08 — I Thirst

**Hook:** *"The One who made every ocean gasped two words on a cross: I thirst."*  
Strong irony; true to John 19:28; excellent ~5s hook.

**CTA:** *"The fountain ran dry for you; come and drink."*  
Strong grace landing (substitution → living water offer). Christ-implicit via prior *"living water"* / thirst thread. **"come and drink"** is invitation, not pressure. Does new work.

**Thread/flow:** thirst pictured → I thirst → fountain dry → drink. Holds. Middle’s *"Psalm twenty-two doesn't foretell that cry"* is a **model honesty line** the set needs elsewhere (cf. 02 hook).

## KJV (quoted spans only)

Per your note, spans were pre-verified. I did not find an altered **word** inside the quoted clauses in the artifact. Two **framing** issues remain:

1. **02 hook** claims *"word for word"* while quoted material differs at *"on the LORD"* / *"in God"* — that is a **rhetorical** not KJV violation, but it is still an accuracy problem.
2. **05:** *"It is finished".* — acceptable if terminal punctuation is treated as outside KJV wording (as your locked notes describe).

---

## Grace / fear / works (set)

No manufactured fear or gain-loss framing detected in the middles. **05** and **01** are cleanly grace-anchored. **02/04/06 CTAs** fail less on *works* than on ** failing to invite to Jesus at all** — they invite to an undefined *"it"* or call to *"answer"* without gospel content. That is **soft**, not heretical, but below charter for CTA-to-Jesus shorts.

---

## VERDICT: REVISE

**TOP FIXES:**
1. **Replace the three thin CTAs** — *"Let it in."* (02), *"Step into it."* (04), *"Answer it."* (06) — with beat-specific, **Jesus-named** grace invitations that **complete each thread** (deliver/love → Christ; grave→family → risen Christ/brethren; nations turn → turn to the Lord/Jesus), and **break the new `[verb] it` template**.
2. **Fix 02’s hook overclaim:** drop *"word for word"*; align the open with *"The same gesture. The same taunt"* so hook and middle don’t contradict on first hearing.
3. **Raise the floor on landing quality to match 05/08:** every CTA should do **new doctrinal work** and name or unmistakably point to **Jesus** — not restate the prior sentence in two words (*"Step into it"* after *"a family"*) or end on an unresolved pronoun (*"Let it in"*).
