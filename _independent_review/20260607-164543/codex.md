# Independent review — codex (OK, 92s)

**Set-Level Finding**
The de-templating solved the old repeated CTA, but introduced a new weakness: several CTAs are now too thin to carry gospel invitation. Exact weak lines: “Let it in,” “Step into it,” “Answer it,” and “Bring Him your broken places.” These could fit almost any devotional short and do not clearly land on Jesus, faith, repentance, or grace.

**Per Short**
01 passes. Hook grips and stays true. CTA “He was stripped so you could be clothed in Him — turn to Him” is grace-anchored and tied to the verse.

02 needs revision. Hook overclaims: “word for word” is too strong because Psalm 22 and Matthew are similar but not identical. CTA “Let it in” is vague and generic; it lands on emotional receptivity, not clearly on Christ.

03 mostly works, but refine. Hook “wasn't His own line” may sound like Jesus was merely borrowing a script rather than truly crying from the cross. CTA has good warmth, but “He was forsaken so that you never will be” needs “in Him” or similar qualification to avoid sounding universal.

04 needs revision. Hook “Everyone stops reading Psalm twenty-two at the cross” is clickbait overclaim. CTA “Step into it” is the weakest in the set: vague, generic, and unclear what “it” means. Also “not ashamed to call you family” should be tied to coming to Christ, not stated flatly to every listener.

05 passes. Hook and CTA are text-specific. “There's nothing left for you to finish; rest in the One who said it is done” is the strongest close in the set.

06 needs revision. Hook is good. CTA “Answer it” is too thin and abstract. The short is about the nations turning to the LORD; the close should invite the listener to turn to the crucified and risen Christ, not merely “answer” a song.

07 needs revision. Hook “autopsy of a crucifixion” is gripping but sensational and over-precise for the quoted lines. CTA “Bring Him your broken places” shifts from atonement to therapeutic language and could be any episode boilerplate.

08 mostly works, but refine. Hook is strong. CTA “come and drink” is biblical, but “The fountain ran dry for you” is theologically risky and too clever; Christ thirsted bodily, but the living fountain did not cease to be life-giving.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Replace the vague CTAs: “Let it in,” “Step into it,” “Answer it,” and “Bring Him your broken places.”
2. Remove hook overclaims: especially “word for word,” “Everyone stops reading,” and “autopsy of a crucifixion.”
3. Add clearer Christ-centred qualification where needed: “in Him,” “turn to Him,” “trust the risen Christ,” or equivalent.
