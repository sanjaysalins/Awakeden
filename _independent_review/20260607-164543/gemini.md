# Independent review — gemini (OK, 50s)

The de-templating effort successfully removed the repetitive "thousand years" and "come to Him" framing, but in doing so, it fell directly into the trap flagged in the brief: replacing a shared phrase with vague, therapeutic boilerplate. You’ve traded a repetitive but explicitly Christian CTA for self-help jargon that fails to land on Christ.

Here is the specific breakdown of the failures:

**02_The_Mockers_Words**
*   **The Flaw:** The CTA is pure therapy-speak. *"It was love they couldn't measure... Let it in."*
*   **Why it fails:** "Let it in" is soft, passive, and entirely divorced from the Person of Christ. It treats the cross as a generalized aura of affection rather than a savior to be trusted. It fails the mandate to "land on JESUS."

**04_Declared_To_The_Brethren**
*   **The Flaw:** The CTA relies on corporate/lifestyle jargon. *"It ends not in a grave, but a family. Step into it."*
*   **Why it fails:** You do not "step into" a family in biblical theology; you are adopted into it by Christ. "Step into it" is meaningless filler that deflates the theological weight of Hebrews 2. 

**06_The_Ends_Of_The_Earth**
*   **The Flaw 1 (Hook Clarity):** *"It opens with one forsaken man dying alone."* There is no antecedent for "It." A listener tuning in on the first second has absolutely no idea what "It" refers to because "the song" isn't mentioned until the next sentence. This fails the "clarity on first hearing" test.
*   **The Flaw 2 (CTA):** *"That song was reaching all the way to you. Answer it."* 
*   **Why it fails:** How does a listener "answer" a song? You've pivoted the object of the CTA from Christ to the Psalm itself. It sounds deep but is structurally nonsensical. 

**03_The_Forsaken_Cry**
*   **The Flaw:** Thread swerve at the landing. *"He went into that dark to bring you home — so run home to the Father who waited."*
*   **Why it fails:** The entire script is focused intently on the Son taking our place in the dark. At the exact moment of the CTA, you drop Jesus and pivot to the Father. While doctrinally acceptable in a broader sense, it violates the brief's specific mandate: "landing on JESUS by invitation." 

**07_The_Body_Foretold**
*   **The Flaw:** Tonal whiplash. The script sets up a brutal, precise forensic examination of a crucifixion (*"autopsy", "every wrenched joint"*) and then suddenly drops into a youth-group cliché: *"Bring Him your broken places."*
*   **Why it fails:** It uses a cheap pun on "broken." You are equating the literal dislocation of the Savior's skeletal structure with the modern emotional concept of being "broken." It’s an unearned, emotionally manipulative leap.

**01, 05, and 08**
These three actually survive the brief. Short 05 (*"rest in the One who said it is done"*) and Short 08 (*"The fountain ran dry for you; come and drink"*) feature strong, one-thread spines with grace-anchored CTAs that actually point to Christ's finished work. Short 01's CTA (*"turn to Him"*) is a bit generic, but it is deeply anchored by the preceding theological setup ("stripped so you could be clothed").

**Summary of the Set**
The variety is superficial. You removed the literal shared words but accidentally created a new, worse template for 02, 04, and 06: the "Three-Word Abstract Imperative" (*Let it in / Step into it / Answer it*). They are thin, lack a Christological object, and must be rewritten.

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Eradicate the vague, three-word abstract CTAs in 02, 04, and 06 ("Let it in," "Step into it," "Answer it"). Rewrite them to land explicitly and actively on Jesus.
2. Fix the missing antecedent in the hook of Short 06. Do not start with "It opens" when the listener doesn't know what "It" is.
3. Correct the tonal whiplash in 07 (bridging literal physical torture to a therapeutic cliché) and the thread-swerve in 03 (pivoting from the Son to the Father at the buzzer).
