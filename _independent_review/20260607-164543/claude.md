# Independent review — claude (OK, 71s)

I reviewed the 8 as a set. Doctrine and KJV are clean; the problem is concentrated in three CTAs, and it's a *set-level* problem.

## The headline finding: de-templating created a NEW shared template

The old close was "Come to Him" ×8. It's gone — but three shorts now share an identical micro-pattern: **terse "[verb] it" imperative, 2–3 words, vague "it" antecedent, Jesus not named.**

- **02 →** "Let it in."
- **04 →** "Step into it."
- **06 →** "Answer it."

These rhyme structurally and each is individually thin. That's the exact regression the brief asked me to watch for: you traded a shared *phrase* for a shared *shape*. Side by side they read as boilerplate, and the other five CTAs (clothed/run home/rest/bring broken/come and drink) prove the engine can do concrete, thread-anchored closes — so these three stand out as under-built.

## Per-short on the three weak CTAs

**06 "Answer it" — weakest, and a doctrinal-aim miss.** The antecedent chain is: "That song was reaching all the way to you. Answer it." So the listener is invited to *answer a song*. The close must land the invitation on **Jesus**, not on a psalm. "Answer it" is vague AND points at the wrong object. This is the one I'd insist on.

**02 "Let it in."** Antecedent is "love… still reaching for you. Let it in." The antecedent is okay (love), but the button is far too light for the weight of the line before it ("the only way to deliver you"). It under-delivers the landing and doesn't name Christ.

**04 "Step into it."** "It ends not in a grave, but a family. Step into it." Antecedent (family) is fine; the close is generic and could sit on any episode. The strong material is the brethren/family thread — the CTA should cash that in concretely.

None of the three is *unsound* — they're thin, vague, and collectively templated.

## Hooks — mostly strong, one residual overlap

Hooks 03 ("wasn't His own line — He was quoting"), 05 (last line/last breath: finished), 07 ("autopsy of a crucifixion"), 08 ("The One who made every ocean gasped two words") are fresh and grip in ~5s, true to verse.

Minor: **01 and 02 are both time-gap prediction hooks** — "Ten centuries before the first nail" and "written down, word for word, a millennium early." Same device twice. Not a blocker (the bodies all lean on "a thousand years" anyway, by design), but if you want maximum variety across the set, vary one of these two off the distance-in-time frame.

Tiny overclaims, all acceptable as vivid language: 07 "autopsy" (the psalm shows a living dying man, not a corpse exam); 04 "Everyone stops reading" (hyperbole). Leave them.

## What's clean

- **Doctrine:** evangelical, Christ-centred, substitution explicit (01 stripped→clothed, 03 "bearing the forsaking that our sin deserved," 05 "nothing left to earn, to prove, or to add," 08 "in your place"). No fear / gain-loss / works framing anywhere.
- **Thread spine:** intact on all 8 — each hook→middle→CTA rides one thread (garments, taunt, forsaken-cry, brethren, finished, nations, body, thirst).
- **Five of eight CTAs** are exemplary: grace-anchored, invitation, thread-specific, name or point clearly at Christ.

## Suggested rewrites (keep grace-anchored, name/point at Christ, kill the "__ it" shape)

- **06:** e.g. "That song was always reaching for you — turn to the One it was singing about." (lands on Jesus, not the song)
- **02:** e.g. "That love is still reaching for you — let Him reach you." (names Him, keeps the reach image)
- **04:** e.g. "He's not ashamed to call you family — come home to your Brother." (cashes the brethren thread)

VERDICT: REVISE
TOP FIXES:
1. Rewrite 02/04/06 — they form a new shared "[verb] it" template (Let it in / Step into it / Answer it): each is thin, vague-antecedent, and none names or clearly points at Jesus.
2. Fix 06 specifically — "Answer it" lands the invitation on "a song," not on Christ; the close must invite to Jesus.
3. Vary one of hooks 01/02 — both are the same "X-years-before-the-cross, written early" device; differentiate to keep the set's hook variety genuine.
