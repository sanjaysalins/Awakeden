# Independent review — grok (OK, 31s)

## Set-level finding (most serious)

A **new shared CTA template** replaced the old one.

Three shorts end on **two-word imperatives + vague “it”**:

| Short | CTA |
|-------|-----|
| 02 | **“Let it in.”** |
| 04 | **“Step into it.”** |
| 06 | **“Answer it.”** |

Same shape: command + pronoun the listener must guess. None names **Jesus**. All three could drop onto almost any gospel short. The brief already flagged this pattern — and it’s here.

The other five CTAs are **specific, Christ-anchored, and tied to the verse thread**. The set is **uneven**, not fully de-templated.

---

## 01 — The Crucifixion Foretold

**Hook:** *“Ten centuries before the first nail, a song already knew how the dying man's clothes would be divided.”*  
Grips fast. Concrete. Stays inside Psalm 22:18 (garments / lots). No overclaim.

**CTA:** *“He was stripped so you could be clothed in Him — turn to Him.”*  
Grace-anchored. Lands on Jesus. “Clothed in Him” fits Galatians 3:27. Thread holds: stripped clothes → clothed in Christ.

**Minor:** Middle still says *“A thousand years later”* while the hook says *“Ten centuries”* — small register shift, not a break.

**Verdict:** PASS (weakest of the strong five, still solid)

---

## 02 — The Mockers' Words

**Hook:** *“The taunts they would scream at the cross were written down, word for word, a millennium early.”*  
**Overclaim.** The script itself shows Matthew is **parallel, not identical** KJV:

- Psalm: *“He trusted on the LORD that he would deliver him: let him deliver him…”*
- Matthew: *“He trusted in God; let him deliver him now…”*

*“Word for word”* in the first ~5 seconds oversells what the middle then corrects. Skeptical listener may feel bait-and-switched.

**CTA:** *“Let it in.”*  
**FAIL on CTA criteria.**  
- “It” = *“love they couldn't measure, and it is still reaching for you”* — abstract on audio.  
- No **Jesus**, no **cross**, no **gospel** in the close.  
- Grace tone in the line before; the CTA itself is **thin invitation**, not doctrinal landing.  
- Pure boilerplate.

**Thread:** Mockers → love stayed on the cross → **“Let it in”** does not finish that arc with a named Christ or a clear gospel act (trust, turn, receive).

**Verdict:** REVISE

---

## 03 — The Forsaken Cry

**Hook:** *“The loneliest sentence Jesus ever spoke wasn't His own line — He was quoting.”*  
Strong ~5s grip. Faithful (Psalm 22:1 on the cross). Clear even with zero Bible background.

**CTA:** *“He went into that dark to bring you home — so run home to the Father who waited.”*  
Grace-anchored. Substitution without earn/prove framing. Father/Jesus implied through “home.” Specific to forsakenness → reunion.

**Minor:** *“run home”* could sound slightly active, but in context it’s **return**, not works — acceptable.

**Verdict:** PASS

---

## 04 — Declared To The Brethren

**Hook:** *“Everyone stops reading Psalm twenty-two at the cross.”*  
Good tension. True to the text’s turn (anguish → praise / brethren). Sets up Hebrews 2:11–12 well.

**CTA:** *“Step into it.”*  
**Weak / boilerplate.**  
- “It” = *“a family”* two lines up — listener must hold that referent.  
- No **Jesus** in the close.  
- Could close a church-invite, community short, anything.  
- After strong material (*“not ashamed to call you family”*), the landing **under-delivers**.

**Thread:** Grave → family in Christ → **“Step into it”** is logically adjacent but **vague**; doesn’t say *believe*, *come to Christ*, *receive the Brother who is not ashamed*.

**Verdict:** REVISE

---

## 05 — He Hath Done This

**Hook:** *“A psalm's last line and Jesus' last breath in John both close on the same note: finished.”*  
Grips. Honest: middle admits *“Different words, in different tongues”* — good fidelity.

**CTA:** *“There's nothing left for you to finish; rest in the One who said it is done.”*  
Best in the set. Anti-works. Grace-anchored. Directly tied to *“It is finished”* / *“he hath done this.”* Not generic.

**Verdict:** PASS

---

## 06 — The Ends Of The Earth

**Hook:** *“It opens with one forsaken man dying alone.”*  
**Weakest hook in the set.**  
- “It” = unidentified for ~5s.  
- Other hooks name Jesus, the psalm, or a concrete image; this one is **generic until the next sentence**.  
- Still true to Psalm 22’s arc (one man → nations).

**CTA:** *“Answer it.”*  
**Boilerplate.**  
- Answer **what**? The song? The nations’ turn? *“That song was reaching all the way to you”* — **“Answer it”** doesn’t say trust Christ, worship the risen Lord, turn to the LORD the verse names.  
- No **Jesus** in the close though the middle is Christ-centred.  
- Slight **pressure** vibe (“answer”) without clarity — not fear, but **manufactured urgency** without content.

**Verdict:** REVISE

---

## 07 — The Body Foretold

**Hook:** *“A king described a body coming apart so precisely it reads like an autopsy of a crucifixion.”*  
Vivid, grips. Faithful to vv. 14–17. *“Autopsy”* is punchy/modern but not doctrinally off.

**CTA:** *“Bring Him your broken places.”*  
Acceptable. Lands on **Him**. Grace invitation, not earn-your-healing. Tied to broken body thread.

**Minor:** Slightly **wellness-generic** — could fit other “Jesus heals your pain” shorts — but still stronger than 02/04/06 because **“Him”** is explicit.

**Verdict:** PASS (borderline)

---

## 08 — I Thirst

**Hook:** *“The One who made every ocean gasped two words on a cross: I thirst.”*  
Excellent ~5s grip. Christ-centred. Honest setup: middle says Psalm 22 doesn’t quote *“I thirst”* verbatim but paints the thirst — no false prophecy claim.

**CTA:** *“The fountain ran dry for you; come and drink.”*  
Strong. Substitution + invitation. Living-water echo (John 4/7). Specific to thirst thread. Not cheesy.

**Verdict:** PASS

## Cross-cutting checks

| Lens | Finding |
|------|---------|
| **Doctrinal soundness** | Evangelical, Christ-centred throughout. No contrarian readings. Substitution, finished work, brethren in Hebrews — all orthodox. |
| **KJV verbatim** | Quoted lines match standard KJV; ellipses are editorial truncation, not word changes. **Hook** on 02 overclaims verbatim match — not a KJV error, but a **truthfulness** issue. |
| **Clarity** | 06 hook and 02/04/06 CTAs hurt **zero-Bible-knowledge** listeners. |
| **Grace-anchored** | No fear/gain-loss/works pressure in middles. **05** is exemplary. Thin CTAs don’t preach works, but they **fail to invite clearly**. |
| **One thread spine** | Middles hold. **02, 04, 06** CTAs **snap the thread** at the last second — strong verse-specific setup, vague landing. |
| **Landing does new work** | **01, 03, 05, 08** yes. **02, 04, 06** repeat the same empty closer shape. |
| **Hook variety** | Real across the set. **06** is the outlier — pronoun opener. |
| **CTA variety** | **Not real** — three-match template undermines the de-template goal. |

VERDICT: REVISE  
TOP FIXES:  
1. Replace **02 / 04 / 06** CTAs — drop the **“[verb] it”** template; each close must **name Jesus or the gospel act** and **complete that short’s thread** (e.g. 02: mockers’ taunt → He stayed for you → **trust the One they mocked**; 04: brethren → **come to the Brother who is not ashamed**; 06: nations turn → **turn to the LORD that song named — the Christ of the cross**).  
2. Fix **02** hook: cut *“word for word”* or change to *“the same taunt”* / *“the same words”* so the opening doesn’t overclaim against Matthew’s wording.  
3. Rewrite **06** hook so **Psalm 22** or **the cross** is in the first line — e.g. lead with *one forsaken man on a cross-shaped prophecy*, not anonymous *“It opens…”*.
